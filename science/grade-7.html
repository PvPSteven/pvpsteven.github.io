Seventh grade science

Here is a list of science skills students learn in seventh grade! These skills are organized into categories, and you can move your mouse over any skill name to preview the skill. To start practicing, just click on any link. IXL will track your score, and you will get immediate feedback as you practice!

Science practices and tools

  1. A.1 Identify steps of the scientific method

Designing experiments

  1. B.1 Identify control and experimental groups
  2. B.2 Identify independent and dependent variables
  3. B.3 Identify the experimental question
  4. B.4 Identify questions that can be investigated with a set of materials
  5. B.5 Understand an experimental protocol about plant growth
  6. B.6 Understand an experimental protocol about diffusion
  7. B.7 Understand an experimental protocol about evaporation

Engineering practices

  1. C.1 Identify parts of the engineering-design process
  2. C.2 Evaluate tests of engineering-design solutions
  3. C.3 Use data from tests to compare engineering-design solutions
  4. C.4 Explore the engineering-design process: going to the Moon!

Density

  1. D.1 Calculate density, mass, and volume

Atoms and molecules

  1. E.1 What are atoms and chemical elements?
  2. E.2 How are substances represented by chemical formulas and models?
  3. E.3 Identify chemical formulas for ball-and-stick models
  4. E.4 Describe the atomic composition of molecules
  5. E.5 Classify elementary substances and compounds using chemical formulas
  6. E.6 Classify elementary substances and compounds using models

Chemical reactions

  1. F.1 Identify reactants and products
  2. F.2 Count atoms and molecules in chemical reactions
  3. F.3 Calculate amounts of reactants or products in chemical reactions
  4. F.4 Describe energy changes in chemical reactions
  5. F.5 Compare physical and chemical changes
  6. F.6 Explore chemical structure and properties: soapmaking
  7. F.7 Explore chemical structure and properties: food flavors

Velocity, acceleration, and forces

  1. G.1 Calculate velocity from time and distance
  2. G.2 Calculate distance from velocity and time
  3. G.3 Calculate time from velocity and distance
  4. G.4 Calculate velocity, distance, and time I
  5. G.5 Calculate velocity, distance, and time II
  6. G.6 Identify whether objects are accelerating
  7. G.7 How does mass affect force and acceleration?
  8. G.8 Predict forces using Newton's third law
  9. G.9 Balanced and unbalanced forces

Kinetic and potential energy

  1. H.1 Identify changes in gravitational potential energy
  2. H.2 Use tables and graphs to identify patterns about kinetic energy
  3. H.3 Explore energy transformations: roller coaster ride
  4. H.4 Explore energy transformations: bike ride

Thermal energy

  1. I.1 Predict heat flow and temperature changes
  2. I.2 Compare thermal energy transfers

Particle motion and energy

  1. J.1 How does particle motion affect temperature?
  2. J.2 Particle motion and changes of state
  3. J.3 How does particle motion affect gas pressure?
  4. J.4 Identify how particle motion affects temperature and pressure

Waves

  1. K.1 Compare amplitudes, wavelengths, and frequencies of waves
  2. K.2 Compare energy of waves

Solutions

  1. L.1 Compare concentrations of solutions
  2. L.2 Diffusion across membranes

Classification and scientific names

  1. M.1 Identify common and scientific names
  2. M.2 Origins of scientific names
  3. M.3 Use scientific names to classify organisms

Biochemistry

  1. N.1 Structure and function: carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids
  2. N.2 Understanding the chemistry of cellular respiration

Cells

  1. O.1 Understanding cells
  2. O.2 Identify functions of plant cell parts
  3. O.3 Identify functions of animal cell parts
  4. O.4 Plant cell diagrams: label parts
  5. O.5 Animal cell diagrams: label parts
  6. O.6 Compare cells and cell parts

Anatomy and physiology

  1. P.1 Organization in the human body: the heart and the circulatory system
  2. P.2 Science literacy: how does the nervous system produce phantom pain?

Genes to traits

  1. Q.1 Inherited and acquired traits: use evidence to support a statement
  2. Q.2 Genetics vocabulary: genotype and phenotype
  3. Q.3 Genetics vocabulary: dominant and recessive
  4. Q.4 Genetic variation in sexual reproduction
  5. Q.5 Complete and interpret Punnett squares
  6. Q.6 Use Punnett squares to calculate ratios of offspring types
  7. Q.7 Use Punnett squares to calculate probabilities of offspring types
  8. Q.8 Genes, proteins, and traits: understanding the genetic code
  9. Q.9 Describe the effects of gene mutations on organisms
  10. Q.10 How do genes and the environment affect plant growth?

Adaptations and natural selection

  1. R.1 How can animal behaviors affect reproductive success? Identify evidence to support a claim
  2. R.2 Introduction to natural selection
  3. R.3 Calculate the percentages of traits in a population
  4. R.4 Calculate the averages of traits in a population
  5. R.5 Construct explanations of natural selection

Fossils

  1. S.1 Compare ages of fossils in a rock sequence

Plant reproduction

  1. T.1 Angiosperm and conifer life cycles
  2. T.2 Moss and fern life cycles

Photosynthesis

  1. U.1 How do plants use and change energy?
  2. U.2 Identify the photosynthetic organism

Ecosystems

  1. V.1 Describe populations, communities, and ecosystems
  2. V.2 Identify ecosystems
  3. V.3 Describe ecosystems

Ecological interactions

  1. W.1 How does matter move in food chains?
  2. W.2 Interpret food webs I
  3. W.3 Interpret food webs II
  4. W.4 Use food chains to predict changes in populations
  5. W.5 Classify symbiotic relationships
  6. W.6 Investigate primary succession on a volcanic island

Conservation

  1. X.1 Coral reef biodiversity and human uses: explore a problem
  2. X.2 Coral reef biodiversity and human uses: evaluate solutions

Natural resources and human impacts

  1. Y.1 Evaluate claims about natural resource use: groundwater
  2. Y.2 Evaluate claims about natural resource use: fossil fuels

Rocks and minerals

  1. Z.1 Identify rocks and minerals
  2. Z.2 Classify rocks as igneous, sedimentary, or metamorphic
  3. Z.3 Label parts of rock cycle diagrams
  4. Z.4 Select parts of rock cycle diagrams

Plate tectonics

  1. AA.1 Label Earth layers
  2. AA.2 Label Earth features at tectonic plate boundaries
  3. AA.3 Describe tectonic plate boundaries around the world

Topographic maps

  1. BB.1 Select parts of a topographic map

Water cycle

  1. CC.1 Label parts of water cycle diagrams
  2. CC.2 Select parts of water cycle diagrams

Weather and climate

  1. DD.1 Use data to describe climates
  2. DD.2 Explore air masses
  3. DD.3 Identify and compare air masses
  4. DD.4 How do air masses form?

Climate change

  1. EE.1 The carbon cycle
  2. EE.2 The greenhouse effect
  3. EE.3 Use data to explain climate change

Natural hazards

  1. FF.1 Analyze natural hazard maps

Astronomy

  1. GG.1 Analyze models of the Earth-Sun-Moon system
  2. GG.2 Identify phases of the Moon
  3. GG.3 What causes the seasons on Earth?
  4. GG.4 Analyze data to compare properties of planets

Units and measurement

  1. HH.1 Choose customary units of distance, mass, and volume
  2. HH.2 Choose metric units of distance, mass, and volume
  3. HH.3 Estimate temperatures
  4. HH.4 Abbreviate length, speed, and acceleration units
  5. HH.5 Abbreviate temperature, mass, and volume units
  6. HH.6 Abbreviate force, energy, and electricity units
