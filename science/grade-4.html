Fourth grade science

Here is a list of science skills students learn in fourth grade! These skills are organized into categories, and you can move your mouse over any skill name to preview the skill. To start practicing, just click on any link. IXL will track your score, and you will get immediate feedback as you practice!

Materials

  1. A.1 Compare properties of objects
  2. A.2 Compare properties of materials

Matter and mass

  1. B.1 Calculate density
  2. B.2 Understand conservation of matter using graphs

States of matter

  1. C.1 Identify and sort solids, liquids, and gases
  2. C.2 Change-of-state diagrams: solid, liquid, and gas
  3. C.3 Heating, cooling, and changes of state

Heat and thermal energy

  1. D.1 Predict heat flow
  2. D.2 Predict temperature changes
  3. D.3 How is temperature related to thermal energy?

Physical and chemical change

  1. E.1 Identify physical and chemical changes
  2. E.2 Compare physical and chemical changes

Mixtures

  1. F.1 Identify mixtures

Force and motion

  1. G.1 Identify directions of forces
  2. G.2 How do balanced and unbalanced forces affect motion?
  3. G.3 How does mass affect force and acceleration?

Electricity

  1. H.1 Introduction to static electricity and charged objects

Magnets

  1. I.1 Identify magnets that attract or repel
  2. I.2 Label magnets that attract or repel
  3. I.3 Compare strengths of magnetic forces

Classification

  1. J.1 Identify living and nonliving things
  2. J.2 Identify mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, and amphibians
  3. J.3 Identify vertebrates and invertebrates
  4. J.4 Use evidence to classify mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, and amphibians
  5. J.5 Use evidence to classify animals
  6. J.6 Describe, classify, and compare kingdoms

Scientific names

  1. K.1 Identify common and scientific names
  2. K.2 Origins of scientific names
  3. K.3 Use scientific names to classify organisms

Animals

  1. L.1 Read and construct animal life cycle diagrams
  2. L.2 Compare animal life cycles
  3. L.3 Body systems: circulation and respiration
  4. L.4 Body systems: digestion
  5. L.5 Body systems: removing waste
  6. L.6 Body systems: perception and motion

Plants

  1. M.1 Classify fruits and vegetables as plant parts
  2. M.2 Identify plant parts and their functions
  3. M.3 How do plants make food?
  4. M.4 Identify flower parts and their functions
  5. M.5 Describe and construct flowering plant life cycles
  6. M.6 Describe and construct conifer life cycles

Adaptations

  1. N.1 Introduction to adaptations
  2. N.2 Animal adaptations: beaks, mouths, and necks
  3. N.3 Animal adaptations: feet and limbs
  4. N.4 Animal adaptations: skins and body coverings

Traits and heredity

  1. O.1 What affects traits? Use observations to support a hypothesis
  2. O.2 Match offspring to parents using inherited traits
  3. O.3 Identify inherited and acquired traits
  4. O.4 Inherited and acquired traits: use evidence to support a statement
  5. O.5 Read a plant pedigree chart
  6. O.6 Read an animal pedigree chart

Cells

  1. P.1 Identify functions of plant cell parts
  2. P.2 Identify functions of animal cell parts
  3. P.3 Plant cell diagrams: identify parts
  4. P.4 Animal cell diagrams: identify parts
  5. P.5 Plant cell diagrams: label parts
  6. P.6 Animal cell diagrams: label parts
  7. P.7 Compare plant and animal cells
  8. P.8 Cell part functions: true or false

Ecosystems

  1. Q.1 Identify ecosystems
  2. Q.2 Describe ecosystems
  3. Q.3 Identify roles in food chains
  4. Q.4 How does matter move in food chains?
  5. Q.5 Interpret food webs

Natural resources

  1. R.1 Evaluate natural energy sources

Rocks and minerals

  1. S.1 Identify minerals using properties
  2. S.2 Identify rocks using properties
  3. S.3 How do sedimentary rocks form?
  4. S.4 Classify rocks as igneous, sedimentary, or metamorphic
  5. S.5 How do rock layers form?

Fossils

  1. T.1 Introduction to fossils
  2. T.2 Identify and classify fossils
  3. T.3 Compare fossils to modern organisms
  4. T.4 Compare ancient and modern organisms: use observations to support a hypothesis
  5. T.5 Interpret evidence from fossils in rock layers

Weather and climate

  1. U.1 Read a thermometer
  2. U.2 Compare temperatures on thermometers
  3. U.3 Collect and graph temperature data
  4. U.4 What's the difference between weather and climate?
  5. U.5 Weather and climate around the world
  6. U.6 Weather or climate? Cite text
  7. U.7 Use climate data to make predictions
  8. U.8 Use data to describe climates

Earth's features

  1. V.1 Describe and graph water on Earth
  2. V.2 Read a topographic map
  3. V.3 Select parts of a topographic map

Engineering practices

  1. W.1 Evaluate multiple design solutions to prevent flooding
  2. W.2 Identify the best design solution to prevent hurricane damage

Units and measurement

  1. X.1 Choose customary units of distance
  2. X.2 Choose metric units of distance
  3. X.3 Choose customary units of mass
  4. X.4 Choose metric units of mass
  5. X.5 Choose customary units of volume
  6. X.6 Choose metric units of volume
  7. X.7 Abbreviate time and length units
  8. X.8 Abbreviate mass and volume units
