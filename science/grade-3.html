Third grade science

Here is a list of science skills students learn in third grade! These skills are organized into categories, and you can move your mouse over any skill name to preview the skill. To start practicing, just click on any link. IXL will track your score, and you will get immediate feedback as you practice!

Materials

  1. A.1 Identify properties of an object
  2. A.2 Compare properties of objects
  3. A.3 Compare properties of materials
  4. A.4 Identify materials in objects
  5. A.5 Identify multiple materials in objects

States of matter

  1. B.1 Classify matter as solid, liquid, or gas
  2. B.2 Identify solids, liquids, and gases
  3. B.3 Sort solids, liquids, and gases

Phase change

  1. C.1 Change-of-state diagrams: solid, liquid, and gas
  2. C.2 Heating, cooling, and changes of state

Heat and thermal energy

  1. D.1 Predict heat flow
  2. D.2 Predict temperature changes
  3. D.3 How is temperature related to thermal energy?

Physical and chemical change

  1. E.1 Identify physical and chemical changes
  2. E.2 Compare physical and chemical changes

Mixtures

  1. F.1 Identify mixtures

Force and motion

  1. G.1 Identify pushes and pulls
  2. G.2 How do balanced and unbalanced forces affect motion?
  3. G.3 How do mass and force affect motion?
  4. G.4 Compare the speeds of moving objects

Electricity

  1. H.1 Introduction to static electricity and charged objects

Magnets

  1. I.1 Identify magnets that attract or repel
  2. I.2 Label magnets that attract or repel
  3. I.3 Compare strengths of magnetic forces

Classification

  1. J.1 Identify living and nonliving things
  2. J.2 Identify plants and animals
  3. J.3 Identify mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, and amphibians
  4. J.4 Identify vertebrates and invertebrates

Animals

  1. K.1 Read animal life cycle diagrams
  2. K.2 Construct animal life cycle diagrams
  3. K.3 Compare stages of an animal's life cycle
  4. K.4 Compare different animals' life cycles
  5. K.5 Benefits of group behavior: North American caribou
  6. K.6 Benefits of group behavior: African wild dogs
  7. K.7 Benefits of group behavior: leaf-cutter ants

Plants

  1. L.1 Classify fruits and vegetables as plant parts
  2. L.2 Identify plant parts and their functions
  3. L.3 How do plants make food?
  4. L.4 Read and construct flowering plant life cycle diagrams
  5. L.5 How do flowering plants make new plants?

Traits

  1. M.1 Observe and compare traits
  2. M.2 What affects traits? Use observations to support a hypothesis

Adaptations

  1. N.1 Introduction to adaptations
  2. N.2 Animal adaptations: beaks, mouths, and necks
  3. N.3 Animal adaptations: feet and limbs
  4. N.4 Animal adaptations: skins and body coverings

Heredity

  1. O.1 Match offspring to parents using inherited traits
  2. O.2 Identify inherited and acquired traits
  3. O.3 Inherited and acquired traits: use evidence to support a statement
  4. O.4 Read a plant pedigree chart
  5. O.5 Read an animal pedigree chart

Ecosystems

  1. P.1 Identify ecosystems
  2. P.2 Describe ecosystems
  3. P.3 Identify roles in food chains
  4. P.4 How does matter move in food chains?
  5. P.5 Interpret food webs

Rocks and minerals

  1. Q.1 Classify rocks and minerals by color, shape, and texture
  2. Q.2 Identify minerals using properties
  3. Q.3 Identify rocks using properties
  4. Q.4 Classify rocks as igneous, sedimentary, or metamorphic

Fossils

  1. R.1 Introduction to fossils
  2. R.2 Compare fossils to modern organisms
  3. R.3 Compare ancient and modern organisms: use observations to support a hypothesis

Weather and climate

  1. S.1 Read a thermometer
  2. S.2 Compare temperatures on thermometers
  3. S.3 Collect and graph temperature data
  4. S.4 What's the difference between weather and climate?
  5. S.5 Weather and climate around the world
  6. S.6 Weather or climate? Cite text
  7. S.7 Use climate data to make predictions
  8. S.8 Use data to describe U.S. climates
  9. S.9 Use data to describe world climates

Earth's features

  1. T.1 Water on Earth

Earth events

  1. U.1 Classify changes to Earth's surface I
  2. U.2 Classify changes to Earth's surface II
  3. U.3 Find evidence of changes to Earth's surface
  4. U.4 Changes to Earth's surface: earthquakes
  5. U.5 Changes to Earth's surface: volcanic eruptions
  6. U.6 Changes to Earth's surface: erosion

Engineering practices

  1. V.1 Solve problems using magnets
  2. V.2 Evaluate multiple design solutions to prevent flooding
  3. V.3 Identify the best design solution to prevent hurricane damage

Units and measurement

  1. W.1 Choose units of time
  2. W.2 Choose customary units of distance
  3. W.3 Choose metric units of distance
  4. W.4 Abbreviate time and length units
  5. W.5 Abbreviate mass and volume units
