Strategies for success:

The growth that I saw in my students last year was monumental. The number of skills a student mastered reflected in their data for district assessments, class work, and standardized tests. About 96% of students passed ISTEP and 84% scored Pass+! We use IXL in a variety of ways each and every day in our classroom. We are a 1:1 school, so we use digital content as a component of daily work and in our rotational models. Students have a personalized "playlist" in which they have skills they set goals for. As students reach their goals, they track data on their list and choose the next skill to master. We have an "IXL Top 10 Leaderboard" where we highlight students who make the most growth each week. Students get super motivated to come to school early, stay late, or work over breaks to see who can be the next student to reach 100, 150, 200, or 250 skills mastered!

Favorite teacher tools:

The fact that students receive immediate feedback is huge, as it allows students to identify misconceptions and correct them on the spot. I love the Real-Time Center as it helps me identify students who may be off task or in need of extra help! I use the Real-Time Center and Skills reports that allow me to easily find groups of students that need assistance with particular concepts.

Making it fun:

After working on a specific skill for a week or two, we introduced a few correlating IXL skills in the form of a competition. Students got out whiteboards or notebooks and headed to the gym or cafeteria. As a grade level, we saw which class could master the most skills in a given time frame. Doing this from time to time was a way of motivating our students and finding a way to change the pace of everyday math. On a weekly basis, we form teams, and students earn points for their teams each time they master a skill. The students loved celebrating one another for reaching goals and earning points for their teams!

This gives me the opportunity to work with small groups on skills they may be struggling with. I also use IXL as homework. I assign my students a weekly goal of one and a half hours per week and I give them specific areas to focus on that reinforce skills we are working on in class. I take the time at the beginning of each school year to show my students how to use IXL and how to learn from their mistakes. I think it is important that they take the time to review the explanation of any problem they may get wrong, and to continue with the skill until they reach mastery and earn a gold medal. I encourage my students to become self-motivated learners and I show them that with IXL, their learning is endless. They have access to all math skills necessary to be successful in 5th grade and they have access to many other skills that they will encounter in future years. I've had so many students get so excited after earning gold medals in the assigned required areas that they move on to other topics. [My students] use IXL to be self-motivated learners and to master many skills that I would not have time to cover within one school year. I also allow students to come to school early and stay late to work on IXL. I am often pleasantly surprised when I have 10 to 15 students offering to stay on a Friday night to master the necessary skills to be successful. It's not uncommon to see me leaving the school as late as 4:30 or 5:00 with a full group of students that has volunteered to stay and complete IXL. I've seen tremendous growth in many of my students using IXL.
