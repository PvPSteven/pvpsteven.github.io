Strategies for success:

I use IXL as a means of tracking students' strengths and areas of difficulty. IXL allows me to focus my lessons according to individual and class needs rather than on the next planned lesson for the day. I also use IXL as a portion of class and homework activities, and as a means of tracking work habits as well as specific math skills. I encourage students, as well as parents, to use the data that IXL gathers to view and monitor their own progress so that they can become proactive learners by celebrating their accomplishments and seeking assistance when troubles arise. Since IXL displays the underlying math concepts for each question, parents often feel empowered to help their child with their math homework. Suddenly, the "new math" difficulties that parents find challenging become easier to understand. Parents often tell me that they enjoy using the software with their children, and they wish that they had had IXL when they were in school. This daily conversation about mathematics and children's education as a whole has helped foster a learning community between our students, our parents, and our school.

Favorite teacher tools:

I appreciate the data that IXL gathers on each student so that I can focus my teaching on skills that are most in need of attention. I also value that achievement is evaluated through the SmartScore system which evaluates skill level according to skills mastered rather than a percentage of right and wrong answers. I appreciate that this program allows my students to work and progress according to their abilities and to find explanations to questions they find difficult when I'm not able to help them at home.

Making it fun:

To encourage students to complete each section, I often allow students who master each section first to choose a book of their liking from our book club. I also use the same reward for each student who has shown the most improvement in each section.
