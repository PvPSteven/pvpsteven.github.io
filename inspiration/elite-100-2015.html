Strategies for success:

IXL is a tremendous part of my classroom instruction. I implement the math component each morning and I also use the program for student differentiation. As the class completes other morning work, I assign students specific IXL math skills to complete. This works well as a daily spiral review of topics learned throughout the school year, as well as a way to focus on topics posing difficulties for particular students. These quick daily assignments allow me to get almost all students on IXL every day. I also assign "Daily Language Arts sessions" to groups of students, particularly during silent sustained reading times. Additionally, I love the fact that I can quickly assign specific sections in both subject areas in class as tests. Since teachers can access student reports and see the results of each student, my time is saved in grading. Over the past several years, I have given my students weekly IXL math homework assignments to improve their math skills. I wholeheartedly believe that using IXL in my classroom has played a vital role in the many student success stories that I have seen each year.

Favorite teacher tools:

I love that the program provides immediate feedback for my students when they enter an incorrect answer. It allows the students to get help while the teacher is occupied with other duties. The detailed reports that teachers can access are also extremely beneficial. This is my favorite math resource in class by far! Whenever I am presented with the opportunity to spread the word of this great program to new people, I do!

Strategies for success:

Every week, I give my 5th graders a goal of doing IXL homework practice for 1.5 hours, including the weekends. On Monday mornings, I pull the prior week's report to see who has reached that goal. I assign their work by writing one skill number on the board and telling them that I want to see them get a gold medal on that skill. This skill is always a reinforcement of what we have been learning in math class. After they receive a gold medal, they are allowed to choose their own skills to work on for the rest of the week. Additionally, I also give my students a goal to do at least 5 hours of IXL over the summer. One of the biggest reasons we've had such success with IXL in our urban school is because we provide many opportunities for students who do not have access to a computer or the Internet. We open our classrooms before and after school for any child who would like to use the school's computers to achieve their IXL goal. At the end of each day, we let students who are waiting for the bus use the laptops in the library for IXL practice. We also open our school one morning per week in the summer for students who wish to do IXL.

Favorite teacher tools:

I use the usage ranking report feature the most. I pull this report every Monday morning to see who achieved their goal for the previous week. I love how it ranks the students by time. I can quickly see who has achieved the 1.5 hours of work I assigned and who did not. The students love to know who was at the top of the list each week. This motivates many of the students to spend more time on IXL, as they all want to be in one of the top slots. I also find the IXL site in general to be extremely easy to align to the units of study that I am teaching in math. The way the skills are broken down and described makes it simple to quickly select the skills I want my students to practice. I write the corresponding letter and number on my whiteboard for all to see.

Making it fun:

Each week, I let my students know if they've reached their assigned goal on IXL. Students who've reached the goal are given a small reward, like a pencil, an eraser, or a classroom privilege. For those who do not meet the goal, I conference with them to find out how I can help them achieve it for the following week. Our school also does IXL recognition at the end of each month and trimester. The students who complete all of their summer work, which includes 5 hours on IXL, can participate in a back-to-school reward (this past year was a school dance). Most importantly, I have high expectations and let students know how much I think practice on IXL can benefit their learning. This has made all the difference in my classroom.

Strategies for success:

We have an hour of technology time each day at school and students spend about 20-30 minutes of that time on their IXL assignment. They also work on it at home. Their IXL assignments are linked to each chapter of our current curriculum. I pick 10 skills that go with each chapter, and the students have about 2 weeks to complete them all. Each student needs to earn 80-100 points on each skill. Their IXL grade is an average of their points, and IXL makes up a third of my students' entire math grade. In the spring, before testing, I have students work at their own pace on 3 different IXL assignments that I have prepared. I think this really helps them do their best on the tests.

Strategies for success:

We are a one-to-one school between students and mini iPads, and IXL has made the transition easy! We use IXL as our daily practice. After a lesson, I model working through the IXL problems. We begin by doing the first 5 together, then I switch to the same lesson that I have previously worked in the "Challenge Zone". As a group we work through the remainder of the problems together. Finally, students work through the skill independently.

Favorite teacher tools:

My favorite IXL features are the ability to differentiate, and the reports. I love that in my classroom I can have students working on the skills that they need. My students range in ability and needs. I can reach the needs of ALL of my students easily through IXL. For example, I have had one student working on preschool skills, another on 1st grade, the majority on the 2nd grade skill, and a few working on 3rd and 4th grade skills. I can fill the gaps of the students who need extra practice and I can push the students who need to be challenged. The IXL reports make it easy to track who has learned the skill and who needs some remediation. With a quick glance, I can see who needs extra instruction and I can provide it immediately without having to grade or group the students myself. Favorite teacher tools:

For my students, the most valuable feature of IXL is that they can work at their own pace and on skills that are at their appropriate level. Targeted practice is the key to learning. Students must be able to work at a level that challenges them without frustration. Since IXL gives students the ability to work on skills that are at their level they learn at a quicker rate and with less frustration. As a teacher, I find the skill proficiency report to be my most valuable tool. As students are working, I am monitoring this report and identifying students who need my assistance. Once I identify a struggling student I am able to go to their side and give them the assistance they need. Using paper, I would never have been able to meet the needs of my struggling students with such speed and precision.
