Skills available for New Jersey pre-K language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: New Jersey Preschool Teaching and Learning Expectations: Language

Language

  • Conventions of Standard English

    • L.PK.1 Begin to understand the conventions of standard English grammar when speaking during interactions and activities.

      • a Print many alphabet letters.

      • b Use frequently occurring nouns and verbs.

        • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (P-M.1)
        • Find the picture that matches the action verb (P-N.1)

      • c Form regular plural nouns.

        • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (P-M.1)

      • d Understand and use question words (e.g., who, what, where, when, why, how).

        • Who, what, when, where, or why? (K-Y.1)

      • e Use frequently occurring prepositions (e.g., to, from, in, out, on, off, for, by, with).

        • Inside and outside (P-P.1)
        • Above and below (P-P.2)
        • Next to and beside (P-P.3)

      • f Begin to speak in complete sentences.

        • Find the complete sentence (K-CC.3)

      • g Understands and can follow simple multi-step directions.

        • What will happen next? (P-K.2)

    • L.PK.2 Begin to understand the simple conventions of standard English grammar during reading and writing experiences throughout the day.

      • c Attempt to write a letter or letters by using scribble-writing, letter-like forms, letter-strings, and invented spelling during writing activities throughout the day.

  • Vocabulary Acquisition and Use

    • L.PK.4 Begin to determine the meaning of new words and phrases introduced through preschool reading and content.

      • a With guidance and support, generate words that are similar in meaning (e.g., rock/stone, happy/glad).

        • Match synonyms (K-Z.2)

    • L.PK.5 With guidance and support, explore word relationships.

      • a Begin to sort familiar objects (e.g., sort a collection of plastic animals into groups: dogs, tigers, and bears).

        • Sort objects into categories (P-R.1)
        • Which one is not like the others? (P-R.2)

      • b Begin to understand opposites of simple and familiar words.

        • Match antonyms to pictures (P-Q.1)

      • c Identify real-life connections between words and their use (e.g., "Tell me the name of a place in the classroom that is noisy or quiet.").

    • L.PK.6 Use words and phrases acquired through conversations, activities and read alouds.
