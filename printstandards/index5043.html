Skills available for Massachusetts fifth-grade language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Massachusetts Curriculum Frameworks: Writing

W Writing

  • Text Types and Purposes

    • W.1 Write opinion pieces on topics or texts, supporting a point of view with reasons and information.

      • W.1.a Introduce a topic or text clearly, state an opinion, and create an organizational structure in which ideas are logically grouped in paragraphs and sections to support the writer's purpose.

        • Identify the purpose of a text (5-B.1)
        • Organize information by main idea (5-K.5)
        • Distinguish facts from opinions (5-L.1)
        • Identify an author's statement of opinion (5-L.2)

      • W.1.b Provide logically ordered reasons that are supported by facts and details.

        • Choose reasons to support an opinion (5-L.3)
        • Identify supporting details in literary texts (5-L.4)
        • Identify supporting details in informational texts (5-L.5)

      • W.1.c Link opinion and reasons using words, phrases, and clauses (e.g., consequently, specifically).

        • Use coordinating conjunctions (5-K.2)
        • Choose the best transition (5-K.3)
        • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (5-II.3)

      • W.1.d Provide a concluding statement or section related to the opinion presented.

    • W.2 Write informative/explanatory texts to examine a topic and convey ideas and information clearly.

      • W.2.a Introduce a topic clearly, provide a general observation and focus, and group related information logically in paragraphs and sections; include text features (e.g., headings), illustrations, and multimedia when useful to aiding comprehension.

        • Put the sentences in order (5-K.1)
        • Organize information by main idea (5-K.5)

      • W.2.b Develop the topic with facts, definitions, concrete details, quotations, or other information and examples related to the topic.

        • Distinguish facts from opinions (5-L.1)
        • Choose reasons to support an opinion (5-L.3)
        • Identify supporting details in literary texts (5-L.4)
        • Identify supporting details in informational texts (5-L.5)

      • W.2.c Link ideas within and across categories of information using words, phrases, and clauses (e.g., in contrast, especially).

        • Use coordinating conjunctions (5-K.2)
        • Choose the best transition (5-K.3)
        • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (5-II.3)

      • W.2.d Use precise language and domain-specific vocabulary to inform about or explain the topic.

        • Order items from most general to most specific (5-K.4)
        • Describe the difference between related words (5-X.1)
        • Positive and negative connotation (5-X.2)
        • Determine the meaning of domain-specific words with pictures (5-Y.4)

      • W.2.e Provide a concluding statement or section related to the information or explanation presented.

    • W.3 Write narratives in prose or poem form to develop experiences or events using effective literary techniques, descriptive details, and clear sequences.

      • W.3.a Orient the reader by establishing a situation and introducing a speaker, narrator, and/or characters; organize an appropriate narrative sequence.

        • Put the sentences in order (5-K.1)

      • W.3.b Use narrative techniques such as dialogue, description, and pacing to develop experiences or events or show responses to situations.

        • Use actions and dialogue to understand characters (5-F.1)
        • Show character emotions and traits (5-M.1)

      • W.3.c Use a variety of transitional words, phrases, and clauses to manage sequence.

        • Put the sentences in order (5-K.1)
        • Choose the best transition (5-K.3)

      • W.3.d Use concrete words and phrases and sensory details to convey experiences or events precisely.

        • Sort sensory details (5-D.1)
        • Identify sensory details (5-D.2)
        • Order items from most general to most specific (5-K.4)
        • Describe the difference between related words (5-X.1)
        • Positive and negative connotation (5-X.2)

      • W.3.e Provide a sense of closure appropriate to the narrated experiences or events.

      • W.3.f For prose narratives, draw on characteristics of traditional or modern genres (e.g., tall tales, myths, mysteries, fantasies, historical fiction) from diverse cultures as models for writing.

      • W.3.g For poems, draw on characteristics of traditional poetic forms (e.g., ballads, couplets) or modern free verse from diverse cultures as models for writing.

        • Label the rhyme scheme (5-J.1)
        • Identify elements of poetry (5-J.2)
