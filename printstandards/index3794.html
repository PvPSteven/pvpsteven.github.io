Skills available for Massachusetts pre-K language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Massachusetts Curriculum Framework (Common Core) (2011): Language

L Language

  • Conventions of Standard English

    • L.MA.1 Demonstrate use of oral language in informal everyday activities.

      • L.MA.1.a (Begins in kindergarten)

      • L.MA.1.b Use frequently occurring nouns and verbs.

        • Choose the two sight words that are the same (P-H.1)
        • Choose the sight word that you hear (P-H.2)
        • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (P-M.1)
        • Find the picture that matches the action verb (P-N.1)

      • L.MA.1.c Form regular plural nouns.

        • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (P-M.1)

      • L.MA.1.d Understand and use question words (e.g., who, what, where, when, why, how).

        • Which could happen in real life? (P-J.1)
        • Which feeling matches the picture? (P-K.1)
        • What will happen next? (P-K.2)
        • Who, what, when, where, or why? (K-Y.1)
        • Is it a telling sentence or an asking sentence? (K-CC.1)

      • L.MA.1.e Use the most frequently occurring prepositions (e.g., to, from, in, out, on, off, for, of, by, with).

        • Choose the two sight words that are the same (P-H.1)
        • Choose the sight word that you hear (P-H.2)
        • Inside and outside (P-P.1)
        • Above and below (P-P.2)
        • Next to and beside (P-P.3)

      • L.MA.1.f Demonstrate the ability to speak in complete sentences.

      • L.MA.1.g Use vocabulary in the Massachusetts Curriculum Framework for Mathematics pre-kindergarten standards to express concepts related to length, area, weight, capacity, and volume.

    • L.2 (Begins in kindergarten)

  • Knowledge of Language

    • L.3 (Begins in grade 2)

  • Vocabulary Acquisition and Use

    • L.MA.4 Ask and answer questions about the meanings of new words and phrases introduced through books, activities, and play.

      • L.MA.4.a With guidance and support, generate words that are similar in meaning (e.g., happy/glad, angry/mad).

        • Match synonyms (K-Z.2)
        • Sort objects into categories (K-AA.1)

      • L.MA.4.b (Begins in kindergarten)

    • L.MA.5 With guidance and support from adults, explore word relationships and nuances of word meanings.

      • Match antonyms to pictures (K-Z.1)
      • Match synonyms (K-Z.2)
      • Sort objects into categories (K-AA.1)

      • L.MA.5.a Demonstrate understanding of concepts by sorting common objects into categories (e.g., sort objects by color, shape, or texture).

        • Sort objects into categories (P-R.1)
        • Which one is not like the others? (P-R.2)

      • L.MA.5.b (Begins in kindergarten)

      • L.MA.5.c Apply words learned in classroom activities to real-life examples (e.g., name places in school that are fun, quiet, or noisy).

      • L.MA.5.d (Begins in kindergarten)

    • L.MA.6 Use words and phrases acquired through conversations, listening to books read aloud, activities, and play.
