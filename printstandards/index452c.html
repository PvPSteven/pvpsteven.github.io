Skills available for Massachusetts fifth-grade social studies standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL social studies skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Massachusetts Concepts and Skills (2003)

History and Geography

  • 1 Identify different ways of dating historical narratives (17th century, seventeenth century, 1600s, colonial period).

  • 2 Interpret timelines of events studied.

    • Jamestown: the early years (5-F.1)
    • Jamestown: growth of a colony (5-F.2)
    • Plymouth (5-F.3)
    • New England colonies: religion and government (5-F.4)
    • The American Revolution: the Thirteen Colonies under British rule (5-G.1)
    • The American Revolution: the Boston Tea Party (5-G.3)
    • The American Revolution: turning the tide of the war (5-G.7)
    • Antebellum Period: slavery and politics part I (5-H.7)
    • World War I: the road to war (5-J.1)
    • World War II: lead-up to war in Europe, Asia (5-J.8)
    • World War II: global events (5-J.9)

  • 3 Observe and identify details in cartoons, photographs, charts, and graphs relating to an historical narrative.

    • Jamestown: the early years (5-F.1)
    • Jamestown: growth of a colony (5-F.2)
    • Plymouth (5-F.3)
    • New England colonies: religion and government (5-F.4)
    • New England colonies: economy and conflict (5-F.5)
    • The American Revolution: the Thirteen Colonies under British rule (5-G.1)
    • The American Revolution: the Boston Tea Party (5-G.3)
    • The American Revolution: turning the tide of the war (5-G.7)
    • The Louisiana Purchase (5-H.1)
    • The Lewis and Clark Expedition (5-H.2)
    • Antebellum Period: economies of the North and South (5-H.5)
    • Antebellum Period: abolitionist and proslavery perspectives (5-H.6)
    • Antebellum Period: slavery and politics part I (5-H.7)
    • World War I: the road to war (5-J.1)
    • World War II: lead-up to war in Europe, Asia (5-J.8)
    • World War II: global events (5-J.9)

  • 4 Use maps and globes to identify absolute locations (latitude and longitude).

    • Identify lines of latitude and longitude (5-A.2)
    • Use lines of latitude and longitude (5-A.3)

  • 5 Identify the location of the North and South Poles, the equator, the prime meridian, Northern, Southern, Eastern, and Western Hemispheres.

  • 6 Distinguish between political and topographical maps and identify specialized maps that show information such as population, income, or climate change.

  • 7 Compare maps of the modern world with historical maps of the world before the Age of Exploration, and describe the changes in 16th and 17th century maps of the world.

Civics and Government

  • 8 Define and use correctly words related to government: citizen, suffrage, rights, representation, federal, state, county, and municipal.

    • Local government (5-K.1)
    • State government (5-K.2)
    • Federal government (5-K.3)
    • The Legislative Branch (6-CC.2)
    • The Executive Branch (6-CC.3)
    • Checks and balances (6-CC.5)
    • The Bill of Rights (6-DD.3)

  • 9 Give examples of the responsibilities and powers associated with major federal and state officials (e.g., the President, chief justice of the U.S. Supreme Court, governor, state senators, and state representatives).

    • State government (5-K.2)
    • Federal government (5-K.3)
    • The Legislative Branch (6-CC.2)
    • The Executive Branch (6-CC.3)
    • The Judicial Branch (6-CC.4)
    • The Bill of Rights (6-DD.3)

  • 10 Explain the structure of the student's city or town government.

    • Local government (5-K.1)

Economics

  • 11 Give examples of the ways people save their money and explain the advantages and disadvantages of each.

    • Banks (5-O.1)

  • 12 Define what an entrepreneur is (a person who has started a business seeking a profit) and give examples from colonial history of an entrepreneur (e.g., Peter Faneuil and Benjamin Franklin).

  • 13 Define profit and describe how profit is an incentive for entrepreneurs.

  • 14 Give examples of how changes in supply and demand affected prices in colonial history (e.g., fur, lumber, fish, and meat).

    • New England colonies: religion and government (5-F.4)
    • Understand quantity supplied and quantity demanded (5-P.1)
    • Understand overall supply and demand (5-P.2)
    • Create and use supply and demand curves (5-P.3)
