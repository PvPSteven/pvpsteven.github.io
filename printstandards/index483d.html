      • K.G.B.4.a Identify and compare like and unlike shapes

        • Circles (K-V.2)
        • Squares and rectangles (K-V.3)
        • Hexagons (K-V.4)
        • Select two-dimensional shapes (K-V.5)
        • Two-dimensional and three-dimensional shapes (K-W.1)
        • Spheres (K-W.3)
        • Cubes (K-W.4)
        • Cones (K-W.5)
        • Cylinders (K-W.6)
        • Select three-dimensional shapes (K-W.7)
        • Shapes of everyday objects I (K-W.9)
        • Shapes of everyday objects II (K-W.10)

      • K.G.B.4.b Identify, analyze and compare shapes of different sizes and orientations.

        • Circles (K-V.2)
        • Squares and rectangles (K-V.3)
        • Hexagons (K-V.4)
        • Select two-dimensional shapes (K-V.5)
        • Count sides and corners (K-V.6)
        • Compare sides and corners (K-V.7)
        • Two-dimensional and three-dimensional shapes (K-W.1)
        • Spheres (K-W.3)
        • Cubes (K-W.4)
        • Cones (K-W.5)
        • Cylinders (K-W.6)
        • Select three-dimensional shapes (K-W.7)
        • Shapes of everyday objects I (K-W.9)
        • Shapes of everyday objects II (K-W.10)

      • K.G.B.4.c Describe similarities and differences, of the parts of the shapes.

      • K.G.B.4.d Name the shapes

        • Name the two-dimensional shape (K-V.1)
        • Name the three-dimensional shape (K-W.2)

    • K.G.B.5 Model shapes in the world by building shapes from components (e.g., sticks and clay balls) and drawing shapes.

      • K.G.B.5.a Use unit blocks and other shapes in the classroom or real world as a model for making 3-dimensional shapes out of clay balls and sticks, or marshmallows and straws, etc.

      • K.G.B.5.b Identify 2-dimensional shapes that make up 3-dimensional shapes.

        • Identify shapes traced from solids (K-W.8)

    • K.G.B.6 Compose simple shapes to form larger shapes.

      • K.G.B.6.a Ability to use concrete materials (e.g. pattern blocks, tangrams, and shape models to build composite figures)

      • K.G.B.6.b Ability to explain how they composed their shape and name what shapes they used to make the composite shape
