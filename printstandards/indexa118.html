Skills available for Massachusetts Spanish standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL Spanish skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Massachusetts Curriculum Frameworks: Stage 1

Communication

  • 1 Students of modern languages will converse in a language other than English to provide and obtain information, express feelings and emotions, and exchange opinions. Students of classical languages will engage in simple oral exchanges and will develop reading skills with discussions of texts conducted in English.

    • 1.1 Greet and respond to greetings

      • Greeting people and saying goodbye (S-1.M)
      • How are you doing? (S-1.N)

    • 1.2 Introduce and respond to introductions

      • Introductions (S-1.O)

    • 1.3 Ask and answer questions

      • Listen and choose the appropriate response to basic questions (S-1.Q)
      • Telling time: provide hours and minutes (S-3.A)
      • Recognize question words: set 1 (S-4.F)
      • Listen to questions about student life and choose the appropriate response (S-4.N)
      • Recognize question words: set 2 (S-6.J)
      • Ask questions related to plans and preferences (S-6.U)
      • Question words: review (S-7.I)

    • 1.4 Make and respond to requests

      • Respond to plans and invitations by giving reasons and excuses (S-7.U)

    • 1.5 Exchange information and knowledge

    • 1.6 Express likes and dislikes

      • Recognize the correct form of gustar + infinitive (S-7.L)
      • Provide the correct form of gustar + infinitive (S-7.M)
      • Write sentences that are true for you about sports, hobbies, and free time (S-7.V)

    • 1.7 Express needs and emotions

      • Provide adjectives that describe personalities (S-2.F)

    • 2.1 Follow directions

      • How are you doing? (S-1.N)

      • Listen and choose the appropriate response to basic questions (S-1.Q)
      • What is the conversation about? (S-1.R)
      • Informal and formal language: tú and usted (S-2.H)
      • Listen and identify the person from the description (S-2.O)
      • Listen to people describe themselves and others (S-2.P)
      • Listen and recognize classes and school supplies (S-3.Q)
      • Listen to people talk about the school day (S-3.R)
      • Read materials related to school and student life (S-3.S)
      • Prepositions of place (S-4.E)
      • Listen to questions about student life and choose the appropriate response (S-4.N)
      • Listen to people talk about their school and everything in it (S-4.O)
      • Listen and match the statement to the image (S-5.R)

      • Read about famous people from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-2.Q)

      • Read about schools and education in the Spanish‑speaking world (S-4.P)

  • 3 Students of modern languages will write and speak in a language other than English to present information, concepts, and ideas on a variety of topics. Presentations in classical language classes will usually take the form of speaking or writing in English.

    • 3.1 Express opinions and ideas

    • 3.2 Express needs and emotions

    • 3.3 Express agreement and disagreement

    • 3.4 Describe people, places, and things

      • What's happening at school? (S-4.R)
      • Write sentences about yourself, your family, or people you know (S-5.V)
      • Write sentences that are true for you about sports, hobbies, and free time (S-7.V)

    • 3.5 Write lists and short notes

    • 3.6 Present information in a brief report

Cultures

  • 4 Students will demonstrate an understanding of the traditions, perspectives, practices, and products of the culture studied, including human commonalities as reflected in history, literature, and the visual and performing arts. In classical language study, discussion and writing will be in English.

    • 4.1 Use appropriate words, phrases, expressions, and gestures in interactions such as greetings, farewells, school routines, and other daily activities.

      • Greeting people and saying goodbye (S-1.M)
      • How are you doing? (S-1.N)
      • Introductions (S-1.O)
      • Informal and formal language: tú and usted (S-2.H)

    • 4.2 Interact appropriately in group cultural activities such as games, storytelling, celebrations, and dramatizations

    • 4.3 Identify distinctive cultural aspects of the target culture presented in stories, dramas, films, and photographs

      • Read about schools and education in the Spanish‑speaking world (S-4.P)

    • 4.4 Identify distinctive cultural products from the target culture such as toys, clothes, foods, currencies, games, traditional crafts, and musical instruments

      • Read about typical meals and foods from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-6.T)

    • 4.5 Identify distinctive contributions made by people in the target culture

      • Read about famous people from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-2.Q)

    • 4.6 Demonstrate knowledge of artistic expression in the target culture by identifying, learning, and performing songs, dances, or memorizing poems; by identifying and making examples of crafts or visual arts using traditional techniques such as brush painting, paper folding, or mosaics

    • 4.7 Demonstrate knowledge of the target culture's geography by naming features such as rivers, mountains, cities, and climate on maps

      • Recognize Spanish‑speaking countries: North and Central America, Europe, and Africa (S-1.I)
      • Name Spanish‑speaking countries: North and Central America, Europe, and Africa (S-1.J)
      • Recognize Spanish‑speaking countries: South America (S-1.K)
      • Name Spanish‑speaking countries: South America (S-1.L)

Comparisons

  • 5 Students will demonstrate an understanding of the nature of language through comparison of the language studied with their own. In classical language study, discussion and writing will be in English.

    • 5.1 Ask and answer questions regarding similar/different phonetic/writing systems used in the target language

    • 5.2 Give examples of ways in which the target language differs from/ is similar to English

      • Read about famous people from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-2.Q)
      • Read materials related to school and student life (S-3.S)
      • Read about schools and education in the Spanish‑speaking world (S-4.P)
      • Cognates and parts of speech (S-5.J)
      • Read about typical meals and foods from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-6.T)
      • Read street interviews about plans, habits, and free time (S-7.T)

    • 5.3 Give examples of borrowed and loan words

    • 5.4 Identify linguistic characteristics of the target language and compare and contrast them with English linguistic characteristics

      • Subject pronouns (S-2.G)
      • Informal and formal language: tú and usted (S-2.H)
      • Sentence structure in affirmative and negative sentences (S-3.P)
      • The prepositions a and de and the contractions al and del (S-4.M)
      • Cognates and parts of speech (S-5.J)

  • 6 Students will demonstrate an understanding of the concept of culture through comparison of the target culture with their own. In classical language study, discussion and writing will be in English.

    • 6.1 Ask and answer questions regarding different forms of communication in the target culture and their own such as signs, symbols, displays, and inscriptions

    • 6.2 Describe patterns of behavior of the target culture, such as celebrations, and compare/contrast them with those of their own culture

    • 6.3 Describe some cultural beliefs and perspectives relating to family, school, and play in both target culture and their own

    • 6.4 Identify and discuss cultural characteristics of the target culture and compare and contrast them to cultural characteristics of their own culture

      • Listen and fill in a form with personal information: letters and numbers (S-1.P)
      • What is the conversation about? (S-1.R)

Connections

  • 7 Students of modern and classical languages will use the target language to reinforce and expand their knowledge of other disciplines and to acquire new information and knowledge. In classical language study, discussion and presentations will be in English.

    • 7.1 Obtain information and knowledge related to other disciplines from sources in the target language

Communities

  • 8 Students will use languages other than English within and beyond the school setting. Students of classical languages will recognize elements of classical languages and ancient cultures in the world around them, and they will share insights derived from their study of classical languages with others within and beyond the classroom setting.

    • 8.1 Apply knowledge of the target language and culture beyond the classroom setting
