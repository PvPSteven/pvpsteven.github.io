Skills available for Massachusetts second-grade language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Massachusetts Curriculum Frameworks: Language

L Language

  • Conventions of Standard English

    • L.1 Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage when writing or speaking; retain and further develop language skills learned in previous grades.

      • L.1.1 Sentence Structure and Meaning

        • L.1.1.a Produce and expand complete simple and compound declarative, interrogative, imperative, and exclamatory sentences and choose among sentence types depending on the meaning to be conveyed.

          • Is it a complete sentence or a fragment? (2-OO.4)
          • Is it a complete sentence or a run-on? (2-OO.5)
          • Is it a complete sentence, a fragment, or a run-on? (2-OO.6)
          • Unscramble the words to make a complete sentence (2-OO.7)

        • L.1.1.b Use adjectives and adverbs in sentences and choose between them depending on what is to be modified.

          • Use sense words (2-VV.1)
          • Choose between adjectives and adverbs (2-VV.8)
          • Is the word an adjective or adverb? (2-VV.9)

      • L.1.2 Word Usage

        • L.1.2.c Use collective nouns and frequently occurring irregular plural nouns.

          • Which word is a noun? (2-PP.2)
          • Identify nouns (2-PP.3)
          • Sort common and proper nouns (2-PP.4)
          • Is the noun singular or plural? (2-PP.9)
          • Form and use irregular plurals (2-PP.10)

        • L.1.2.d Use reflexive pronouns.

          • Choose between personal and reflexive pronouns (2-QQ.9)
          • Use reflexive pronouns (2-QQ.10)

        • L.1.2.e Form and use the past tense of frequently occurring irregular verbs.

          • Form and use the irregular past tense: set 1 (2-TT.8)
          • Form and use the irregular past tense: set 2 (2-TT.9)
          • Form and use the irregular past tense: set 3 (2-TT.10)
          • Form and use the irregular past tense: set 4 (2-TT.11)
          • To be: use the correct past tense form (2-TT.14)

    • L.2 Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English capitalization, punctuation, and spelling when writing.

      • L.2.a Print upper- and lowercase letters legibly and fluently.

      • L.2.b Capitalize holidays, product names, and geographic names.

        • Capitalizing days, months, and holidays (2-ZZ.2)
        • Capitalizing the names of places and geographic features (2-ZZ.3)

      • L.2.c Use commas in greetings and closings of letters.

        • Greetings and closings of letters (2-ZZ.6)

      • L.2.d Use an apostrophe to form contractions and frequently occurring possessives.

        • Form the singular or plural possessive (2-PP.12)
        • Form pronoun-verb contractions (2-XX.2)
        • Form contractions with "not" (2-XX.3)
        • Use pronoun-verb contractions (2-XX.4)
        • Use contractions with "not" (2-XX.5)

      • L.2.e Generalize learned spelling patterns when writing words (e.g., cage → badge; boy → boil).

        • Complete the words with silent letters (2-C.11)
        • Complete the word with the correct diphthong: oi, oy, ou, ow (2-J.2)
        • Spell the soft g or soft c word (2-L.4)
        • Form regular plurals with -s and -es (2-PP.6)
        • Use regular plurals with -s and -es (2-PP.7)
        • Form and use the regular past tense (2-TT.3)
        • Form pronoun-verb contractions (2-XX.2)
        • Form contractions with "not" (2-XX.3)
        • Use pronoun-verb contractions (2-XX.4)
        • Use contractions with "not" (2-XX.5)

      • L.2.f Consult reference materials, including beginning dictionaries, as needed to check and correct spellings.

        • Use guide words (2-NN.4)

      • L.2.g Demonstrate understanding that context determines whether the writer uses a numeral or a written number (e.g., numerals in 1 + 3 = 4, but written words in "When I was one, I was just begun, / When I was two, I was still quite new" from A. A. Milne's poem "Now We Are Six").

  • Knowledge of Language

    • L.3 Use knowledge of language and its conventions when writing, speaking, reading, or listening.

      • L.3.a Compare formal and informal uses of English.

  • Vocabulary Acquisition and Use

    • L.4 Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases based on grade 2 reading and content, choosing flexibly from an array of strategies.

      • L.4.a Use sentence-level context as a clue to the meaning of a word or phrase.

        • Find synonyms in context (2-HH.2)
        • Find antonyms in context (2-HH.5)
        • Use context to identify the meaning of a word (2-MM.1)

      • L.4.b Determine the meaning of the new word formed when a known prefix is added to a known word (e.g., happy/unhappy, tell/retell).

        • Identify base words, prefixes, and suffixes (2-EE.2)
        • Determine the meaning of a word with pre-, re-, or mis- (2-EE.3)
        • Use the prefixes pre-, re-, and mis- (2-EE.4)

      • L.4.c Use a known root word as a clue to the meaning of an unknown word with the same root (e.g., addition, additional).

        • Determine the meaning of a word with -ful or -less (2-EE.5)
        • Prefixes and suffixes: review (2-EE.6)

      • L.4.d Use knowledge of the meaning of individual words to predict the meaning of compound words (e.g., birdhouse, lighthouse, housefly; bookshelf, notebook, bookmark).

        • Form and use compound words (2-FF.3)

      • L.4.e Use glossaries and beginning dictionaries, both print and digital, to determine or clarify the meaning of words and phrases.

        • Use guide words (2-NN.4)
        • Use dictionary entries (2-NN.5)

      • L.4.f Recognize and use appropriately abbreviations related to grade-level content or commonly used in everyday life (e.g., a.m., p.m.)

        • Abbreviate days of the week (2-AAA.1)
        • Abbreviate months of the year (2-AAA.2)

      • L.4.g Recognize and use appropriately symbols related to grade-level content or commonly used in everyday life (e.g., $, ¢).

    • L.5 Demonstrate understanding of word relationships and nuances in word meanings.

      • L.5.a Identify real-life connections between words and their use (e.g., describe foods that are spicy or juicy).

        • Sort words into categories (2-GG.1)
        • Multiple-meaning words with pictures (2-JJ.1)
        • Shades of meaning with pictures (2-KK.1)
        • Use action verbs (2-RR.1)
        • Use sense words (2-VV.1)
        • Does the adjective tell you what kind or how many? (2-VV.2)
        • Select the best preposition to match the picture (2-WW.1)

      • L.5.b Distinguish shades of meaning among closely related verbs (e.g., toss, throw, hurl) and closely related adjectives (e.g., thin, slender, skinny, scrawny).

        • Shades of meaning with pictures (2-KK.1)
        • Find the words with related meanings (2-KK.2)
        • Describe the difference between related words (2-KK.3)
        • Order related words based on meaning (2-KK.4)

    • L.6 Use words and phrases acquired through conversations, activities in the grade 2 curriculum, reading and being read to, and responding to texts, including using adjectives and adverbs to describe.

      • Form and use compound words (2-FF.3)
      • Use the correct homophone (2-II.3)
      • Shades of meaning with pictures (2-KK.1)
      • Use sense words (2-VV.1)
      • Select the best preposition to match the picture (2-WW.1)
      • Select the best preposition to complete the sentence (2-WW.2)
