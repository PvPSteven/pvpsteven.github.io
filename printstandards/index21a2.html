Skills available for New Jersey pre-K language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: New Jersey Preschool Teaching and Learning Expectations: Reading: Foundational Skills

Reading: Foundational Skills

  • Print Concepts

    • RF.PK.1 Begin to demonstrate understanding of basic features of print.

      • a Follow words from left to right, top to bottom, page by page.

        • Identify book parts and features (P-I.1)

      • b Recognize that spoken words can be written and read.

        • Choose the two words that are the same (P-C.1)
        • Find a word in a sentence (P-C.3)

      • c Recognize that words are separated by spaces.

        • Choose the sentence that is spaced correctly (P-C.2)

      • d Recognize and name many upper and lower case letters of the alphabet.

        • Find the letter in the alphabet: lowercase (P-A.1)
        • Find the letter in the alphabet: uppercase (P-A.2)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: lowercase (P-A.3)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: uppercase (P-A.4)
        • Frequently confused letters: find the letter (P-A.5)
        • Frequently confused letters: find all the letters (P-A.6)

  • Phonological Awareness

    • RF.PK.2 Demonstrate understanding of spoken words and begin to understand syllables and sounds (phonemes).

      • a Recognize and produce simple rhyming words.

        • Choose the words that have a given ending (P-D.1)
        • Choose the picture that rhymes with the word (P-D.2)

      • b Segment syllables in spoken words by clapping out the number of syllables.

        • How many syllables does the word have? (K-E.1)
        • Which word has more syllables? (K-E.2)
        • Sort by the number of syllables (K-E.3)

      • c Identify many initial sounds of familiar words.

        • Identify the first sound in a word (P-E.2)
        • Put the sounds in order (P-E.3)
        • Which two words start with the same sound? (P-F.1)
        • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)

  • Phonics and Word Recognition

    • RF.PK.3 Demonstrate an understanding of beginning phonics and word skills.

      • a Associates many letters (consonants and vowels as ready) with their names and their most frequent sounds.

        • Find the letter in the alphabet: lowercase (P-A.1)
        • Find the letter in the alphabet: uppercase (P-A.2)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: lowercase (P-A.3)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: uppercase (P-A.4)
        • Frequently confused letters: find the letter (P-A.5)
        • Frequently confused letters: find all the letters (P-A.6)
        • Choose the letter that matches the consonant sound (P-F.4)
        • Find the short a word (P-G.1)
        • Find the short e word (P-G.2)
        • Find the short i word (P-G.3)
        • Find the short o word (P-G.4)
        • Find the short u word (P-G.5)

      • c Recognize their name in print as well as other familiar print in the environment.

        • Find a word in a sentence (P-C.3)

  • Fluency

    • RF.PK.4 Begin to engage in a variety of texts with purpose and understanding.
