Skills available for Massachusetts third-grade language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Massachusetts Curriculum Frameworks: Writing

W Writing

  • Text Types and Purposes

    • W.1 Write opinion pieces on topics or texts, supporting an opinion with reasons.

      • W.1.a Introduce the topic or text they are writing about, state an opinion, and create an organizational structure that lists reasons.

        • Identify the author's purpose: passages (3-B.2)
        • Organize information by main idea (3-L.3)
        • Distinguish facts from opinions (3-P.1)
        • Identify an author's statement of opinion (3-P.2)
        • Complete the opinion passage with an example (3-P.4)
        • Complete the opinion passage with a reason (3-P.5)
        • Complete the opinion-reason-example table (3-P.6)

      • W.1.b Provide reasons that support the opinion.

        • Choose reasons to support an opinion (3-P.3)
        • Complete the opinion passage with a reason (3-P.5)
        • Complete the opinion-reason-example table (3-P.6)

      • W.1.c Use linking words and phrases (e.g., because, therefore, since, for example) to connect opinion and reasons.

        • Use coordinating conjunctions (3-O.3)
        • Use subordinating conjunctions (3-O.4)
        • Choose the best transition (3-O.5)
        • Use linking words to complete a passage (3-O.6)

      • W.1.d Provide a concluding statement or section.

    • W.2 Write informative/explanatory texts to examine a topic and convey ideas and information clearly.

      • W.2.a Introduce a topic and group-related information together; include illustrations when useful to aiding comprehension.

        • Identify the author's purpose: passages (3-B.2)
        • Organize information by main idea (3-L.3)
        • Distinguish facts from opinions (3-P.1)
        • Identify an author's statement of opinion (3-P.2)
        • Complete the opinion passage with an example (3-P.4)
        • Complete the opinion passage with a reason (3-P.5)
        • Complete the opinion-reason-example table (3-P.6)

      • W.2.b Develop the topic with facts, definitions, and details.

        • Distinguish facts from opinions (3-P.1)
        • Choose reasons to support an opinion (3-P.3)

      • W.2.c Use linking words and phrases (e.g., also, another, and, more, but) to connect ideas within categories of information.

        • Use coordinating conjunctions (3-O.3)
        • Use subordinating conjunctions (3-O.4)
        • Choose the best transition (3-O.5)
        • Use linking words to complete a passage (3-O.6)
        • Combine sentences: subjects and predicates (3-R.1)

      • W.2.d Provide a concluding statement or section.

    • W.3 Write narratives in prose or poem form to develop experiences or events using effective literary techniques, descriptive details, and clear sequences.

      • W.3.a Establish a situation and introduce a speaker, narrator, and/or characters; organize an appropriate narrative sequence.

        • Put the sentences in order (3-L.1)
        • Choose topic sentences for narrative paragraphs (3-M.2)
        • Add descriptive details to sentences (3-Q.1)

      • W.3.b Use dialogue and descriptions of actions, thoughts, and feelings to develop experiences or events or show responses to situations.

        • Use actions and dialogue to understand characters (3-F.2)
        • Add descriptive details to sentences (3-Q.1)
        • Show character emotions and traits (3-Q.2)

      • W.3.c Use figurative language to suggest images.

        • Sort sensory details (3-D.1)
        • Identify sensory details (3-D.2)
        • Similes with pictures (3-E.1)
        • Determine the meanings of similes (3-E.2)

      • W.3.d Use temporal words and phrases to signal order where appropriate.

        • Put the sentences in order (3-L.1)
        • Identify time-order words (3-O.1)
        • Use time-order words (3-O.2)

      • W.3.e Provide a sense of closure.

      • W.3.f For poems, use words and phrases that form patterns of sound (e.g., rhyme, repetition of sounds within words or within lines) to create meaning or effect.

        • Spell rhyming words to answer riddles (3-K.1)
        • Label the rhyme scheme (3-K.2)
