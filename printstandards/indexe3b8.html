Skills available for Maryland pre-K math standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL math skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Maryland Model for School Readiness

PK.1.0 Students will algebraically represent, model, analyze, or solve mathematical or real-world problems involving patterns or functional relationships.

  • PK.1.A Patterns and Functions

    • PK.1.A.2 Identify, copy, and extend non-numeric patterns

      • PK.1.A.2.a Match patterns kinesthetically such as: clap/snap/clapÂ…

      • PK.1.A.2.b Recognize simple patterns

      • PK.1.A.2.c Represent simple repeating patterns using no more than 2 different objects, and different actions in the core of the pattern

        • Color patterns (P-H.1)
        • Size patterns (P-H.2)
        • Shape patterns (P-H.3)

      • PK.1.A.2.d Continue a simple pattern

        • What comes next? (P-H.4)

      • PK.1.A.2.e Create a simple pattern of 2 different objects when given the rule

      • PK.1.A.2.f Identify patterns in real-world situations

  • PK.1.B Expression, Equations, and Inequalities

    • PK.1.B.2 Identify inequalities

      • PK.1.B.2.a Explore relationships by comparing groups of no more than 5 objects to determine more or less

        • More (P-E.2)
        • Fewer (P-E.3)
        • Fewer and more - compare by counting (P-E.4)
        • Compare in a mixed group (P-E.5)

PK.2.0 Students will apply the properties of one-, two-, or three-dimensional geometric figures to describe reason, or solve problems about shape, size, position, or motion of objects.

  • PK.2.A Plane Geometric Figures

    • PK.2.A.1 Recognize and use the attributes of plane geometric figures

      • PK.2.A.1.a Sort objects by one attribute such as: shape, color, and size

        • Different (P-G.1)
        • Same (P-G.2)
        • Same and different (P-G.3)
        • Classify and sort by color (P-G.5)
        • Classify and sort by shape (P-G.6)
        • Squares (P-K.3)

      • PK.2.A.1.b Name the attributes of plane figures such as: shape, color, size

        • Classify shapes by color (P-G.4)

      • PK.2.A.1.c Match triangles, circles, and squares

        • Circles (P-K.2)
        • Squares (P-K.3)
        • Triangles (P-K.4)
        • Circles, squares, and triangles (P-K.6)

      • PK.2.A.1.d Identify triangles, circles, and squares in the environment

  • PK.2.B Solid Geometric Figure

    • PK.2.B.1 Recognize and use the attributes of solid geometric figures

      • PK.2.B.1.a Sort objects by one attribute such as: size, shape, weight, length

        • Name the solid shape (P-L.2)
        • Spheres (P-L.3)
        • Cubes (P-L.4)
        • Cones (P-L.5)
        • Cylinders (P-L.6)
        • Select solid shapes (P-L.7)

      • PK.2.B.1.b Find solid figures in the environment

        • Shapes of everyday objects I (K-W.9)
        • Shapes of everyday objects II (K-W.10)

  • PK.2.E Transformations

    • PK.2.E.1 Begin to recognize a transformation

      • PK.2.E.1.a Tell position by using words such as: over, under, above, on, next to, below, beside, behind

        • Inside and outside (P-F.1)
        • Left and right (P-F.2)
        • Left, middle, and right (P-F.3)
        • Top and bottom (P-F.4)
        • Top, middle, and bottom (P-F.5)
        • Above and below (P-F.6)
        • Beside and next to (P-F.7)

      • PK.2.E.1.b Recognize a slide using concrete materials

PK.3.0 Students will identify attributes, units or systems of measurements or apply a variety of techniques, formulas, tools or technology for determining measurements.

  • PK.3.A Measurement Units

    • PK.3.A.1 Recognize and use measurement attributes

      • PK.3.A.1.a Demonstrate an understanding of comparative attributes such as: bigger, smaller, longer, shorter, lighter, heavier, shorter, taller, hotter, colder

        • Long and short (P-I.1)
        • Tall and short (P-I.2)
        • Light and heavy (P-I.3)
        • Holds more or less (P-I.4)
        • Wide and narrow (P-I.5)
        • Compare size and weight (P-I.6)

      • PK.3.A.1.b Compare and describe objects according to a single attribute

        • Long and short (P-I.1)
        • Tall and short (P-I.2)
        • Light and heavy (P-I.3)
        • Holds more or less (P-I.4)
        • Wide and narrow (P-I.5)
        • Compare size and weight (P-I.6)

  • PK.3.B Measurement Tools

    • PK.3.B.1 Measure in non-standard units

      • PK.3.B.1.a Measure length of objects

        • Measure using objects (K-S.6)

      • PK.3.B.1.b Explore the capacity of containers

        • Holds more or less (P-I.4)

      • PK.3.B.1.c Explore the weight of objects

        • Light and heavy (P-I.3)

PK.4.0 Students will collect, organize, display, analyze, or interpret data to make decisions or predictions.

  • PK.4.A Data Displays

    • PK.4.A.1 Explore and display data

      • PK.4.A.1.a Explore data by answering a yes/no question

      • PK.4.A.1.b Display data on real graphs

        • Making graphs (K-Q.1)

      • PK.4.A.1.c Display data on picture graphs

        • Making graphs (K-Q.1)

  • PK.4.B Data Analysis

    • PK.4.B.1 Analyze data

      • PK.4.B.1.a Talk about data from real graphs to answer a question such as: Which category has the most?

        • Interpreting graphs (K-Q.2)

PK.6.0 Students will describe, represent, or apply numbers or their relationships or will estimate or compute using mental strategies, paper/pencil, or technology.

  • PK.6.A Knowledge of number

    • PK.6.A.1 Apply knowledge of whole numbers

      • PK.6.A.1.a Build concept of number

        • Learn to count - up to 3 (P-A.1)
        • Learn to count - up to 5 (P-B.1)
        • Learn to count - up to 10 (P-C.1)

      • PK.6.A.1.b Show an understanding of quantity

        • Represent numbers - up to 3 (P-A.8)
        • Represent numbers - up to 5 (P-B.8)
        • Represent numbers - up to 10 (P-C.8)

      • PK.6.A.1.c Construct relationships based on quantity

      • PK.6.A.1.d Use classroom experiences to indicate same, more, or less

      • PK.6.A.1.e Count and discuss quantity

        • Count objects - up to 3 (P-A.2)
        • Count dots - up to 3 (P-A.3)
        • Count shapes - up to 3 (P-A.4)
        • Count on ten frames - up to 3 (P-A.6)
        • Count objects - up to 5 (P-B.2)
        • Count dots - up to 5 (P-B.3)
        • Count shapes - up to 5 (P-B.4)
        • Count on ten frames - up to 5 (P-B.6)
        • Count objects - up to 10 (P-C.2)
        • Count dots - up to 10 (P-C.3)
        • Count shapes - up to 10 (P-C.4)
        • Count on ten frames - up to 10 (P-C.6)

      • PK.6.A.1.f Use concrete materials to build sets 0 to 5

        • Count using stickers - up to 3 (P-A.5)
        • Show numbers on ten frames - up to 3 (P-A.7)
        • Count using stickers - up to 5 (P-B.5)
        • Show numbers on ten frames - up to 5 (P-B.7)

      • PK.6.A.1.g Match a numeral to a set 0 to 5

        • Represent numbers - up to 3 (P-A.8)
        • Represent numbers - up to 5 (P-B.8)

      • PK.6.A.1.h Count to 10

      • PK.6.A.1.i Use ordinal words to indicate position such as: first, next, last

PK.7.0 Students demonstrate the process of mathematics by making connections and applying reasoning to solve and to communicate their findings.

  • PK.7.A Problem solving

    • PK.7.A.1 Apply a variety of concepts, processes, and skills to solve problems

      • PK.7.A.a Identify the question in the problem

      • PK.7.A.b Decide if enough information is present to solve the problem

      • PK.7.A.c Make a plan to solve a problem

      • PK.7.A.d Apply a strategy, i.e., draw a picture, guess and check, finding a pattern, writing an equation

      • PK.7.A.e Select a strategy, i.e., draw a picture, guess and check, finding a pattern, writing an equation

      • PK.7.A.f Identify alternative ways to solve a problem

      • PK.7.A.g Show that a problem might have multiple solutions or no solution

      • PK.7.A.h Extend the solution of a problem to a new problem situation

  • PK.7.B Reasoning

    • PK.7.B.1 Justify ideas or solutions with mathematical concepts or proofs

      • PK.7.B.1.a Use inductive or deductive reasoning

      • PK.7.B.1.b Make or test generalizations

      • PK.7.B.1.c Support or refute mathematical statements or solutions

      • PK.7.B.1.d Use methods of proofs, i.e., direct, indirect, paragraph, or contradiction

  • PK.7.C Communication

    • PK.7.C.1 Present mathematical ideas using words, symbols, visual displays, or technology

      • PK.7.C.1.a Use multiple representations to express concepts or solutions

      • PK.7.C.1.b Express mathematical ideas orally

      • PK.7.C.1.c Explain mathematically ideas in written form

      • PK.7.C.1.d Express solutions using concrete materials

        • Add with pictures - sums up to 5 (K-I.1)
        • Add with pictures - sums up to 10 (K-J.1)

      • PK.7.C.1.e Express solutions using pictorial, tabular, graphical, or algebraic methods

        • Making graphs (K-Q.1)

      • PK.7.C.1.f Explain solutions in written form

      • PK.7.C.1.g Ask questions about mathematical ideas or problems

      • PK.7.C.1.h Give or use feedback to revise mathematical thinking

  • PK.7.D Connections

    • PK.7.D.1 Relate or apply mathematics within the discipline, to other disciplines, and to life

      • PK.7.D.1.a Identify mathematics within the discipline, to other disciplines, and to life

      • PK.7.D.1.b Identify mathematical concepts in relationships to other disciplines

      • PK.7.D.1.c Identify mathematical concepts in relationship to life

      • PK.7.D.1.d Use the relationship among mathematical concepts to learn other mathematical concepts
