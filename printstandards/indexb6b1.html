Skills available for Massachusetts second-grade social studies standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL social studies skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Massachusetts Curriculum Frameworks (2003)

2.1 On a map of the world, locate all the continents: North America, South America, Europe, Asia, Africa, Australia, and Antarctica.

  • Identify oceans and continents (2-A.4)
  • Select oceans and continents (2-A.5)
  • Name oceans and continents (2-A.6)

2.2 Locate the current boundaries of the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

2.3 Locate the oceans of the world: the Arctic, Atlantic, Indian, Pacific, and Southern Oceans.

  • Identify oceans and continents (2-A.4)
  • Select oceans and continents (2-A.5)
  • Name oceans and continents (2-A.6)

2.4 Locate five major rivers in the world: the Mississippi, Amazon, Volga, Yangtze, and Nile.

2.5 Locate major mountains or mountain ranges in the world such as the Andes, Alps, Himalayas, Mt. Everest, Mt. McKinley, and the Rocky mountains.

2.6 Explain the difference between a continent and a country and give examples of each.

2.7 On a map of the world, locate the continent, regions, or the countries from which students, their parents, guardians, grandparents, or other relatives or ancestors came. With the help of family members and the school librarian, describe traditional food, customs, sports and games, and music of the place they came from.
