Skills available for Florida second-grade language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Language Arts Florida Standards (LAFS): Reading Standards for Informational Text

Reading Standards for Informational Text

  • 1 Key Ideas and Details

    • LACC.2.RI.1.1 Ask and answer such questions as who, what, where, when, why, and how to demonstrate understanding of key details in a text.

      • Read and understand informational passages (2-V.1)

    • LACC.2.RI.1.2 Identify the main topic of a multiparagraph text as well as the focus of specific paragraphs within the text.

      • Determine the topic and purpose of informational passages (2-S.2)

    • LACC.2.RI.1.3 Describe the connection between a series of historical events, scientific ideas or concepts, or steps in technical procedures in a text.

  • 2 Craft and Structure

    • LACC.2.RI.2.4 Determine the meaning of words and phrases in a text relevant to a grade 2 topic or subject area.

      • Find synonyms in context (2-HH.2)
      • Find antonyms in context (2-HH.5)
      • Use context to identify the meaning of a word (2-MM.1)

    • LACC.2.RI.2.5 Know and use various text features (e.g., captions, bold print, subheadings, glossaries, indexes, electronic menus, icons) to locate key facts or information in a text efficiently.

      • Use text features (2-W.1)
      • Use guide words (2-NN.4)
      • Use dictionary entries (2-NN.5)

    • LACC.2.RI.2.6 Identify the main purpose of a text, including what the author wants to answer, explain, or describe.

      • Identify the purpose of a text (2-S.1)
      • Determine the topic and purpose of informational passages (2-S.2)
      • Choose the text that matches the writer's purpose (2-AA.1)

  • 3 Integration of Knowledge and Ideas

    • LACC.2.RI.3.7 Explain how specific images (e.g., a diagram showing how a machine works) contribute to and clarify a text.

    • LACC.2.RI.3.8 Describe how reasons support specific points the author makes in a text.

      • Determine the topic and purpose of informational passages (2-S.2)

    • LACC.2.RI.3.9 Compare and contrast the most important points presented by two texts on the same topic.

      • Compare and contrast in informational passages (2-U.1)

  • 4 Range of Reading and Level of Text Complexity

    • LACC.2.RI.4.10 By the end of year, read and comprehend informational texts, including history/social studies, science, and technical texts, in the grades 2–3 text complexity band proficiently, with scaffolding as needed at the high end of the range.

      • Determine the topic and purpose of informational passages (2-S.2)
      • Read and understand informational passages (2-V.1)
