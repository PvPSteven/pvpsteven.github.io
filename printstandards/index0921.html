Skills available for Maryland kindergarten language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Common Core State Standards: Language

L.K Language

  • Conventions of Standard English

    • L.K.1 Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage when writing or speaking.

      • L.K.1.a Print many upper- and lowercase letters.

      • L.K.1.b Use frequently occurring nouns and verbs.

        • Find the picture that matches the action verb (K-W.2)
        • Is the noun a person, animal, place, or thing? (K-EE.1)
        • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (K-EE.2)
        • Find the action verb (K-FF.1)
        • Complete the sentence with an action verb to match the picture (K-FF.2)

      • L.K.1.c Form regular plural nouns orally by adding /s/ or /es/ (e.g., dog, dogs; wish, wishes).

        • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (K-EE.2)

      • L.K.1.d Understand and use question words (interrogatives) (e.g., who, what, where, when, why, how).

        • Who, what, when, where, or why? (K-Y.1)

      • L.K.1.e Use the most frequently occurring prepositions (e.g., to, from, in, out, on, off, for, of, by, with).

        • Inside and outside, above and below, next to and beside (K-X.1)
        • Choose the best location word to match the picture (K-X.2)

      • L.K.1.f Produce and expand complete sentences in shared language activities.

        • Find the complete sentence (K-CC.3)

    • L.K.2 Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English capitalization, punctuation, and spelling when writing.

      • L.K.2.a Capitalize the first word in a sentence and the pronoun I.

        • Capitalize the first letter of a sentence (K-DD.1)
        • Capitalize the pronoun "I" (K-DD.2)

      • L.K.2.b Recognize and name end punctuation.

        • Identify and use end marks (K-CC.2)

      • L.K.2.c Write a letter or letters for most consonant and short-vowel sounds (phonemes).

        • Choose the letter that matches the consonant sound (K-H.4)
        • Which consonant blend does the word start with? (K-I.1)
        • Which consonant blend does the word end with? (K-I.4)
        • Choose the correct digraph (K-I.8)
        • Identify the short vowel sound in a word (K-O.1)

      • L.K.2.d Spell simple words phonetically, drawing on knowledge of sound-letter relationships.

        • Put the sounds in order (K-G.3)
        • Complete the word with the right initial consonant blend (K-I.2)
        • Complete the word with the right final consonant blend (K-I.5)
        • Choose the word that matches the picture: -ss, -ll, -ff, -zz, -ck (K-I.7)
        • Complete the short a word (K-J.3)
        • Complete the short e word (K-K.3)
        • Complete the short i words (K-L.3)
        • Complete the short o word (K-M.3)
        • Complete the short u word (K-N.3)
        • Spell the short vowel word (K-O.3)

  • Knowledge of Language

  • Vocabulary Acquisition and Use

    • L.K.4 Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases based on kindergarten reading and content.

      • L.K.4.a Identify new meanings for familiar words and apply them accurately (e.g., knowing duck is a bird and learning the verb to duck).

        • Multiple-meaning words with pictures (K-BB.1)

      • L.K.4.b Use the most frequently occurring inflections and affixes (e.g., -ed, -s, re-, un-, pre-, -ful, -less) as a clue to the meaning of an unknown word.

        • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (K-EE.2)

    • L.K.5 With guidance and support from adults, explore word relationships and nuances in word meanings.

      • L.K.5.a Sort common objects into categories (e.g., shapes, foods) to gain a sense of the concepts the categories represent.

        • Sort objects into categories (K-AA.1)
        • Which one is not like the others? (K-AA.2)

      • L.K.5.b Demonstrate understanding of frequently occurring verbs and adjectives by relating them to their opposites (antonyms).

        • Match antonyms to pictures (K-Z.1)

      • L.K.5.c Identify real-life connections between words and their use (e.g., note places at school that are colorful).

        • Find the picture that matches the action verb (K-W.2)
        • Choose the best location word to match the picture (K-X.2)
        • Sort objects into categories (K-AA.1)
        • Multiple-meaning words with pictures (K-BB.1)

      • L.K.5.d Distinguish shades of meaning among verbs describing the same general action (e.g., walk, march, strut, prance) by acting out the meanings.

    • L.K.6 Use words and phrases acquired through conversations, reading and being read to, and responding to texts.

      • Choose the best location word to match the picture (K-X.2)
