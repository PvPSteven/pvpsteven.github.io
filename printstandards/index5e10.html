Skills available for Connecticut kindergarten language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Common Core State Standards: Reading Foundational Skills

RF.K Foundational Skills

  • Print Concepts

    • RF.K.1 Demonstrate understanding of the organization and basic features of print.

      • RF.K.1.a Follow words from left to right, top to bottom, and page by page.

        • Find a word in a sentence (K-C.3)
        • Identify book parts and features (K-R.1)

      • RF.K.1.b Recognize that spoken words are represented in written language by specific sequences of letters.

        • Choose the two words that are the same (K-C.1)
        • Identify the first, second, and last sound in a word (K-G.2)
        • Put the sounds in order (K-G.3)

      • RF.K.1.c Understand that words are separated by spaces in print.

        • Choose the sentence that is spaced correctly (K-C.2)
        • Find a word in a sentence (K-C.3)

      • RF.K.1.d Recognize and name all upper- and lowercase letters of the alphabet.

        • Find letters in the alphabet (K-A.1)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: lowercase (K-A.2)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: uppercase (K-A.3)
        • Identify frequently confused letters (K-A.4)

  • Phonological Awareness

    • RF.K.2 Demonstrate understanding of spoken words, syllables, and sounds (phonemes).

      • RF.K.2.a Recognize and produce rhyming words.

        • Choose the words that have a given ending (K-F.1)
        • Choose the picture that rhymes with the word (K-F.2)
        • Which word does not rhyme? (K-F.3)
        • Complete the rhyme (K-F.4)

      • RF.K.2.b Count, pronounce, blend, and segment syllables in spoken words.

        • How many syllables does the word have? (K-E.1)
        • Which word has more syllables? (K-E.2)
        • Sort by the number of syllables (K-E.3)

      • RF.K.2.c Blend and segment onsets and rimes of single-syllable spoken words.

        • Choose the words that have a given ending (K-F.1)
        • Blend the sounds together to make a word (K-G.1)
        • Complete the short a word (K-J.3)
        • Complete the short e word (K-K.3)
        • Complete the short i words (K-L.3)
        • Complete the short o word (K-M.3)
        • Complete the short u word (K-N.3)

      • RF.K.2.d Isolate and pronounce the initial, medial vowel, and final sounds (phonemes) in three-phoneme (consonant-vowel-consonant, or CVC) words. (This does not include CVCs ending with /l/, /r/, or /x/.)

        • Identify the first, second, and last sound in a word (K-G.2)
        • Put the sounds in order (K-G.3)
        • Which two words start with the same sound? (K-H.1)
        • Find all the words that start with a given sound (K-H.2)
        • Which two words end with the same sound? (K-H.3)
        • Which letter does the word start with? (K-H.5)
        • Which letter does the word end with? (K-H.6)
        • Choose the correct digraph (K-I.8)
        • Identify the short vowel sound in a word (K-O.1)

      • RF.K.2.e Add or substitute individual sounds (phonemes) in simple, one-syllable words to make new words.

        • Complete the short a word (K-J.3)
        • Complete the short e word (K-K.3)
        • Complete the short i words (K-L.3)
        • Complete the short o word (K-M.3)
        • Complete the short u word (K-N.3)
        • Identify the short vowel sound in a word (K-O.1)

  • Phonics and Word Recognition

    • RF.K.3 Know and apply grade-level phonics and word analysis skills in decoding words.

      • RF.K.3.a Demonstrate basic knowledge of one-to-one letter-sound correspondences by producing the primary sound or many of the most frequent sounds for each consonant.

        • Choose the letter that matches the consonant sound (K-H.4)
        • Which letter does the word start with? (K-H.5)
        • Which letter does the word end with? (K-H.6)
        • Choose the correct digraph (K-I.8)

      • RF.K.3.b Associate the long and short sounds with common spellings (graphemes) for the five major vowels.

        • Find the short a word (K-J.1)
        • Choose the short a word that matches the picture (K-J.2)
        • Complete the short a word (K-J.3)
        • Choose the short a sentence that matches the picture (K-J.4)
        • Find the short e word (K-K.1)
        • Choose the picture that matches the short e word (K-K.2)
        • Complete the short e word (K-K.3)
        • Choose the short e sentence that matches the picture (K-K.4)
        • Find the short i word (K-L.1)
        • Choose the short i word that matches the picture (K-L.2)
        • Complete the short i words (K-L.3)
        • Choose the short i sentence that matches the picture (K-L.4)
        • Find the short o word (K-M.1)
        • Choose the short o word that matches the picture (K-M.2)
        • Complete the short o word (K-M.3)
        • Choose the short o sentence that matches the picture (K-M.4)
        • Find the short u word (K-N.1)
        • Choose the picture that matches the short u word (K-N.2)
        • Complete the short u word (K-N.3)
        • Choose the short u sentence that matches the picture (K-N.4)
        • Identify the short vowel sound in a word (K-O.1)
        • Complete the word with the right short vowel (K-O.2)
        • Spell the short vowel word (K-O.3)
        • Choose the word that has a different vowel sound (K-P.1)
        • Find the long a word (K-P.2)
        • Find the long e word (K-P.3)
        • Find the long i word (K-P.4)
        • Find the long o word (K-P.5)
        • Find the long u word (K-P.6)
        • Sort short and long vowel words (K-P.7)

      • RF.K.3.c Read common high-frequency words by sight (e.g., the, of, to, you, she, my, is, are, do, does).

        • Choose the two sight words that are the same (K-Q.1)
        • Choose the sight word that you hear (K-Q.2)
        • Complete the sentence with the correct sight word (K-Q.3)

      • RF.K.3.d Distinguish between similarly spelled words by identifying the sounds of the letters that differ.

        • Does the word start with a consonant blend? (K-I.3)
        • Does the word end with a consonant blend? (K-I.6)
        • Choose the short a word that matches the picture (K-J.2)
        • Choose the short i word that matches the picture (K-L.2)
        • Choose the short o word that matches the picture (K-M.2)

  • Fluency

    • RF.K.4 Read emergent-reader texts with purpose and understanding.

      • Choose the short a sentence that matches the picture (K-J.4)
      • Choose the short e sentence that matches the picture (K-K.4)
      • Choose the short i sentence that matches the picture (K-L.4)
      • Choose the short o sentence that matches the picture (K-M.4)
      • Choose the short u sentence that matches the picture (K-N.4)
      • Complete the sentence with the correct short vowel word (K-O.4)
      • Read questions with short vowel words (K-O.5)
      • Complete the sentence with the correct sight word (K-Q.3)
      • What am I? (K-U.3)
