2 Cultures: Gain Knowledge and Understanding of the Cultures of the World.

  • 2.1 Students develop an understanding of the relationship between the practices and perspectives of Hispanic cultures.

    • 2.1.1 Use appropriate gestures and oral expressions for greetings, leave-takings and common classroom interactions

      • 2.1.1.1 Differences between formal and informal relationships; Ways of expressing respect in their own language and the Spanish; Basic gestures

        • Greeting people and saying goodbye (S-1.M)
        • How are you doing? (S-1.N)
        • Introductions (S-1.O)
        • Informal and formal language: tú and usted (S-2.H)

    • 2.1.2 Understand different cultural activities

      • 2.1.2.2 Games, songs and birthday celebrations; Regional dances Holiday practices; Recognition of traditional dress and items connected to holidays or events

  • 2.2 Students demonstrate an understanding of the relationship between the products and perspectives of the different Hispanic cultures.

    • 2.2.1 Observe, identify and/or describe tangible products from Spanish-speaking cultures

      • 2.2.1.1 Clothing, toys, dwellings, city layouts, foods, musical instruments etc.

        • Read about typical meals and foods from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-6.T)

    • 2.2.2 Identify, experience, hear, or read about expressive products of Spanish speakers

      • 2.2.2.2 Songs, literature, rhymes, legends, folklore, tongue twisters

    • 2.2.3 Recognize common themes, ideas or perspectives of groups of Spanish speakers

      • 2.2.3.3 Friendships, individual and family responsibilities, personal space

4 Comparisons: Develop Insight into the Nature of Language and Culture.

  • 4.1 Students demonstrate understanding of the nature of language through comparisons between the language studied and their own.

    • 4.1.1 Cite and use examples of words that are borrowed in Spanish and in English

      • 4.1.1.1 Why languages may need to borrow and adopt words

    • 4.1.2 Realize that cognates enhance comprehension of spoken and written Spanish

      • 4.1.2.2 Commonly-occurring cognates and words ending in -ción, -dad, -ero, -tad, -mente

        • Read about famous people from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-2.Q)
        • Read materials related to school and student life (S-3.S)
        • Read about schools and education in the Spanish‑speaking world (S-4.P)
        • Cognates and parts of speech (S-5.J)
        • Read about typical meals and foods from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-6.T)
        • Read street interviews about plans, habits, and free time (S-7.T)

    • 4.1.3 Show awareness of the existence of idiomatic expressions in English and Spanish

      • 4.1.3.3 Common idiomatic expressions in English and in Spanish

        • Weather and seasons (S-1.H)
        • Acabar de + infinitive (S-6.Q)
        • Select the use of tener or tener que (S-7.P)

    • 4.1.4 Demonstrate an awareness of formal and informal forms of language and expressions of politeness

      • 4.1.4.4 Differences of register (tú vs. usted; vosotros vs. Uds.) and common expressions of politeness and titles of address

        • How are you doing? (S-1.N)
        • Subject pronouns (S-2.G)
        • Informal and formal language: tú and usted (S-2.H)

    • 4.1.5 Begin to recognize differences and similarities between the sound and writing systems of their own language and Spanish

      • 4.1.5.5 Alphabet, sounds of letters and use of accent marks for syllable stress

  • 4.2 Students demonstrate understanding of the concept of culture through comparisons between the cultures studied and their own.

    • 4.2.1 Demonstrate an awareness that gestures are an important part of communication and that gestures may differ between English and Spanish

      • 4.2.1.1 The meaning of basic gestures in English and Spanish

    • 4.2.2 Compare and contrast tangible products of Hispanic cultures and their own

      • 4.2.2.2 Understand the differences between the cultural products of their own country and those of Hispanic cultures (e.g. fútbol/football; chupacabra/boogeyman)
