Skills available for Connecticut pre-K language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Connecticut Early Learning and Development Standards

A Early learning experiences will support children to understand language (receptive language).

  • Word Comprehension

    • L.60.1 Understand an increasing variety and specificity of words for objects, actions and attributes encountered in both real and symbolic contexts

      • Use color words (P-L.1)
      • Use number words: one to five (P-L.2)
      • Use number words: six to ten (P-L.3)
      • Use number words: one to ten (P-L.4)
      • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (P-M.1)
      • Find the picture that matches the action verb (P-N.1)
      • Compare pictures using adjectives (P-O.1)
      • Inside and outside (P-P.1)
      • Above and below (P-P.2)
      • Next to and beside (P-P.3)
      • Match antonyms to pictures (P-Q.1)
      • Sort objects into categories (P-R.1)
      • Which one is not like the others? (P-R.2)

    • L60.2 Determine the meanings of unknown words/concepts using the context of conversations, pictures or concrete objects

  • Language Comprehension

    • L.60.3 Understand increasingly complex sentences that include 3-4 concepts (e.g., "Plants are living things that will not survive without soil, sunlight and water.")

C Early learning experiences will support children to use language for social interaction.

  • Conventions of Conversation

    • L.60.9 Initiate, maintain and end conversations by repeating what other person says and/or by asking questions

  • Language for Interaction

    • L.60.10 Use language to share ideas and gain information

D Early learning experiences will support children to gain book appreciation and knowledge.

  • Interest and Engagement with Books

    • L.60.11 Independently choose to "read" books and select a variety of texts including fiction and

  • Understanding of Stories or Information (Stories or information may be shared through oral storytelling, sharing of pictures and/or books)

E Early learning experiences will support children to gain knowledge of print and its uses.

  • Book Concepts

    • L.60.15 Know how print is read (e.g., left to right, top to bottom, front to back or according to convention of home language)

      • Identify book parts and features (P-I.1)

    • L.60.16 Know that books have titles, authors, illustrators or photographers

      • Identify book parts and features (P-I.1)

    • L.60.17 Recognize words as a unit of print and that letters are grouped to form words

      • Choose the two words that are the same (P-C.1)
      • Choose the sentence that is spaced correctly (P-C.2)
      • Find a word in a sentence (P-C.3)
      • Choose the two sight words that are the same (P-H.1)

  • Print Concepts

    • L.60.18 Identify some familiar printed words out of context

    • L.60.19 Begin to use awareness of letter sounds along with pictures to read words in text

      • Find a word in a sentence (P-C.3)
      • Blend the sounds together to make a word (P-E.1)

  • Letter Recognition

    • L.60.20 Recognize and name known letters of the alphabet in familiar and unfamiliar words

      • Find the letter in the alphabet: lowercase (P-A.1)
      • Find the letter in the alphabet: uppercase (P-A.2)
      • Choose the letter that you hear: lowercase (P-A.3)
      • Choose the letter that you hear: uppercase (P-A.4)
      • Frequently confused letters: find the letter (P-A.5)
      • Frequently confused letters: find all the letters (P-A.6)
      • Choose the lowercase letter that matches: easier letters (P-B.1)
      • Choose the lowercase letter that matches: harder letters (P-B.2)
      • Find all the lowercase letters (P-B.3)
      • Choose the uppercase letter that matches: easier letters (P-B.4)
      • Choose the uppercase letter that matches: harder letters (P-B.5)
      • Find all the uppercase letters (P-B.6)

    • L.60.21 Make some letter-sound connections

      • Identify the first sound in a word (P-E.2)
      • Put the sounds in order (P-E.3)
      • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)
      • Choose the letter that matches the consonant sound (P-F.4)
      • Find the short a word (P-G.1)
      • Find the short e word (P-G.2)
      • Find the short i word (P-G.3)
      • Find the short o word (P-G.4)
      • Find the short u word (P-G.5)

F Early learning experiences will support children to develop phonological awareness.

  • Phonological Awareness

    • L.60.22 Produce rhyming words or words that have same initial sound

      • Choose the words that have a given ending (P-D.1)
      • Choose the picture that rhymes with the word (P-D.2)
      • Which two words start with the same sound? (P-F.1)
      • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)

    • L.60.23 Recognize which words in a set of words begin with the same sound

      • Which two words start with the same sound? (P-F.1)
      • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)

    • L.60.24 Distinguish syllables in words

      • How many syllables does the word have? (K-E.1)
      • Which word has more syllables? (K-E.2)
      • Sort by the number of syllables (K-E.3)

G Early learning experiences will support children to convey meaning through drawing, letters and words.

  • Drawing and Writing

    • L.60.25 Draw original stories with a beginning, middle and end

    • L.60.26 Use early developmental spelling. May use one letter for the initial or final sound to represent whole word
