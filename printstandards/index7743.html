Skills available for Massachusetts eighth-grade language arts standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL language arts skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Massachusetts Curriculum Frameworks: Writing

W Writing

  • Text Types and Purposes

    • W.1 Write arguments (e.g., essays, letters to the editor, advocacy speeches) to support claims with clear reasons and relevant evidence.

      • W.1.a Introduce claim(s), acknowledge and distinguish the claim(s) from alternate or opposing claims, and organize the reasons and evidence logically in paragraphs and sections.

        • Identify author's purpose (8-C.1)
        • Organize information by main idea (8-J.2)
        • Distinguish facts from opinions (8-K.2)

      • W.1.b Support claim(s) with logical reasoning and relevant evidence, using accurate, credible sources and demonstrating an understanding of the topic or text.

        • Choose evidence to support a claim (8-K.3)
        • Identify supporting details in informational texts (8-K.4)
        • Identify supporting details in literary texts (8-K.5)
        • Classify logical fallacies (8-K.6)

      • W.1.c Use words, phrases, and clauses to create cohesion and clarify the relationships among claim(s), counterclaims, reasons, and evidence.

        • Transitions with conjunctive adverbs (8-J.3)
        • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (8-KK.1)

      • W.1.d Establish and maintain a style appropriate to audience and purpose (e.g., formal for academic writing).

        • Which sentence is more formal? (8-C.2)

      • W.1.e Provide a concluding statement or section that follows from and supports the argument presented.

    • W.2 Write informative/explanatory texts (e.g., essays, oral reports, biographical feature articles) to examine a topic and convey ideas, concepts, and information through the selection, organization, and analysis of relevant content. d.

      • W.2.a Introduce a topic clearly, previewing what is to follow; use paragraphs and sections to organize ideas, concepts, and information into broader categories; include text features (e.g., headings), graphics (e.g., charts, tables), and multimedia when useful to aiding comprehension.

        • Compare and contrast (8-D.1)
        • Match causes and effects in informational texts (8-D.2)
        • Match problems with their solutions (8-D.3)
        • Identify text structures (8-D.4)
        • Order topics from broadest to narrowest (8-J.1)
        • Organize information by main idea (8-J.2)

      • W.2.b Develop the topic with relevant, well-chosen facts, definitions, concrete details, quotations, or other information and examples.

        • Distinguish facts from opinions (8-K.2)
        • Choose evidence to support a claim (8-K.3)
        • Identify supporting details in informational texts (8-K.4)
        • Identify supporting details in literary texts (8-K.5)
        • Classify logical fallacies (8-K.6)

      • W.2.c Use appropriate and varied transitions to create cohesion and clarify the relationships among ideas and concepts.

        • Transitions with conjunctive adverbs (8-J.3)
        • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (8-KK.1)

      • W.2.d Use precise language and domain-specific vocabulary to inform about or explain the topic.

        • Order topics from broadest to narrowest (8-J.1)
        • Describe the difference between related words (8-T.1)
        • Positive and negative connotation (8-T.2)
        • Determine the meaning of domain-specific words with pictures (8-X.1)

      • W.2.e Establish and maintain a style appropriate to audience and purpose (e.g., formal for academic writing).

        • Which sentence is more formal? (8-C.2)

      • W.2.f Provide a concluding statement or section that follows from and supports the information or explanation presented.

    • W.3 Write narratives to develop experiences or events using effective literary techniques, relevant descriptive details, and well-structured sequences.

      • W.3.a Engage and orient the reader by establishing a context and point of view and introducing a narrator and/or characters; organize an appropriate narrative sequence.

        • Identify the narrative point of view (8-E.2)

      • W.3.b Use narrative techniques, such as dialogue, pacing, description, and reflection, to develop experiences, events, and/or characters.

        • Identify sensory details (8-E.1)

      • W.3.c Use a variety of transition words, phrases, and clauses to convey sequence, signal shifts from one time frame or setting to another, and show the relationships among experiences and events.

        • Transitions with conjunctive adverbs (8-J.3)

      • W.3.d Use precise words and phrases and relevant descriptive details to convey a tone (the writer's attitude toward the subject: e.g., humorous, serious, or ironic) and to convey experiences or events.

        • Compare passages for tone (8-C.3)
        • Identify sensory details (8-E.1)
        • Describe the difference between related words (8-T.1)
        • Positive and negative connotation (8-T.2)

      • W.3.e Provide a conclusion that follows from and reflects on the narrated experiences or events.

  • Research to Build and Present Knowledge

    • W.7 Conduct short as well as more sustained research projects to answer a question (including a self-generated question), drawing on several sources and generating additional related, focused questions that allow for multiple avenues of exploration.

    • W.8 When conducting research, gather relevant information from multiple print and digital sources, using search terms effectively; assess the credibility and accuracy of each source; and quote or paraphrase the data and conclusions of others while avoiding plagiarism and following a standard format for citation.

      • Determine the main idea (8-A.1)

      • Identify supporting details in informational texts (8-K.4)
      • Identify supporting details in literary texts (8-K.5)
      • Recognize the parts of a Works Cited entry (MLA 7th edition) (8-P.1)
      • Recognize the parts of a Works Cited entry (MLA 8th edition) (8-P.2)
