Skills available for Delaware Spanish standards

Standards are in bold, followed by a list of the IXL Spanish skills that are aligned to that standards. Students can practice these skills online at www.ixl.com.

Standards: Delaware World-Readiness Standards for Learning Languages: Novice Low

1 Communication: Learners communicate effectively in more than one language in order to function in a variety of situations and for multiple purposes.

  • 1.1 Interpersonal Communication: Learners interact and negotiate meaning in spoken, signed, or written conversations to share information, reactions, feelings, and opinions.

    • 1.1.1 communicate on some very familiar topics using single words and phrases.

      • 1.1.1.a greet peers

        • Greeting people and saying goodbye (S-1.M)
        • How are you doing? (S-1.N)
        • Listen and choose the appropriate response to basic questions (S-1.Q)
        • Informal and formal language: tú and usted (S-2.H)

      • 1.1.1.b introduce self to someone

        • Introductions (S-1.O)
        • Informal and formal language: tú and usted (S-2.H)

      • 1.1.1.c answer a few simple questions

        • Listen and choose the appropriate response to basic questions (S-1.Q)

  • 1.2 Interpretive Communication: Learners understand, interpret, and analyze what is heard, read, or viewed on a variety of topics.

    • 1.2.L Listening

      • 1.2.L.1 recognize a few memorized words and phrases when they hear them spoken.

        • 1.2.L.1.a occasionally identify the sound of a character or a word

          • The Spanish alphabet (S-1.A)
          • Listen and fill in a form with personal information: letters and numbers (S-1.P)

        • 1.2.L.1.b occasionally understand isolated words that have been memorized, particularly when accompanied by gestures or pictures

          • Listen and identify the person from the description (S-2.O)
          • Listen to people describe themselves and others (S-2.P)
          • Listen and recognize classes and school supplies (S-3.Q)
          • Listen to people talk about the school day (S-3.R)

    • 1.2.R Reading

      • 1.2.R.1 recognize a few letters or characters. They can identify a few memorized words and phrases when they read.

        • 1.2.R.1.a recognize a few letters or characters

        • 1.2.R.1.b connect some words, phrases, or characters to their meanings

          • Numbers 0‑10 (S-1.B)
          • Numbers 11‑20 (S-1.C)
          • Numbers 21‑31 (S-1.D)
          • Recognize nouns to identify people (S-2.A)
          • Recognize adjectives that describe appearance (S-2.C)
          • Recognize adjectives that describe personalities (S-2.E)
          • Recognize school subjects (S-3.C)
          • Recognize school supplies (S-3.E)
          • Recognize ‑ar verbs related to school (S-3.G)
          • Common ‑ar verbs (S-3.K)
          • Recognize things in the classroom (S-4.A)
          • Recognize places in school (S-4.C)
          • Recognize immediate family members and pets (S-5.A)
          • Recognize extended family members (S-5.C)
          • Adjectives for feelings or states of being: recognize the correct word (S-5.E)
          • Recognize numbers: 30‑100 (S-5.G)
          • Common ‑er and ‑ir verbs (S-5.I)
          • Recognize meals and types of food (S-6.A)
          • Recognize food items (S-6.C)
          • Recognize drinks (S-6.E)
          • Recognize fruits and vegetables (S-6.G)
          • Verbs for talking about food, eating, and drinking (S-6.I)
          • Recognize day‑to‑day activities (S-7.A)
          • Recognize pastimes and hobbies (S-7.C)
          • Recognize sports (S-7.E)
          • Recognize people, places, and things associated with sports (S-7.G)

  • 1.3 Presentational Communication: Learners present information, concepts, and ideas to inform, explain, persuade, and narrate on a variety of topics using appropriate media and adapting to various audiences of listeners, readers, or viewers.

    • 1.3.S Speaking

      • 1.3.S.1 present information about themselves and some other very familiar topics using single words or memorized phrases.

        • 1.3.S.1.a recite words and phrases that they have learned

        • 1.3.S.1.b state the names of familiar people, places, and objects in pictures and posters using words or memorized phrases

        • 1.3.S.1.c introduce themselves to a group.

        • 1.3.S.1.d recite short memorized phrases, parts of poems, and rhymes

    • 1.3.W Writing

      • 1.3.W.1 copy some familiar words, characters, or phrases.

        • 1.3.W.1.a copy some characters or letters and words that they see on the wall or board, in a book, or on the computer

        • 1.3.W.1.b write words and phrases that they have learned

          • Numbers 0‑10 (S-1.B)
          • Numbers 11‑20 (S-1.C)
          • Numbers 21‑31 (S-1.D)
          • Days of the week (S-1.E)
          • Months of the year (S-1.F)
          • Provide nouns to identify people (S-2.B)
          • Provide adjectives that describe appearance (S-2.D)
          • Provide adjectives that describe personalities (S-2.F)
          • Name school subjects (S-3.D)
          • Name school supplies (S-3.F)

          • Name immediate family members and pets (S-5.B)
          • Name extended family members (S-5.D)
          • Adjectives for feelings or states of being: provide the correct word (S-5.F)
          • Provide numbers: 30‑100 (S-5.H)
          • Name meals and types of food (S-6.B)
          • Name food items (S-6.D)
          • Name drinks (S-6.F)
          • Name fruits and vegetables (S-6.H)
          • Name day‑to‑day activities (S-7.B)
          • Name pastimes and hobbies (S-7.D)
          • Name sports (S-7.F)
          • Name people, places, and things associated with sports (S-7.H)

        • 1.3.W.1.c label familiar people, places, and objects in pictures and posters

          • Recognize Spanish‑speaking countries: North and Central America, Europe, and Africa (S-1.I)
          • Name Spanish‑speaking countries: North and Central America, Europe, and Africa (S-1.J)
          • Recognize Spanish‑speaking countries: South America (S-1.K)
