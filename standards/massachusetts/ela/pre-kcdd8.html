RF Reading Foundational Skills

  • Print Concepts

    • RF.1 With guidance and support, demonstrate understanding of the organization and basic features of printed and written text: books, words, letters, and the alphabet.

      • RF.1.a Handle books respectfully and appropriately, holding them right-side-up and turning pages one at a time from front to back.

        • Identify book parts and features (P-I.1)

      • RF.1.d Recognize and name some uppercase letters of the alphabet and the lowercase letters in their own name.

        • Find the letter in the alphabet: lowercase (P-A.1)
        • Find the letter in the alphabet: uppercase (P-A.2)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: lowercase (P-A.3)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: uppercase (P-A.4)
        • Frequently confused letters: find the letter (P-A.5)
        • Frequently confused letters: find all the letters (P-A.6)
        • Choose the lowercase letter that matches: easier letters (P-B.1)
        • Choose the lowercase letter that matches: harder letters (P-B.2)
        • Find all the lowercase letters (P-B.3)
        • Choose the uppercase letter that matches: easier letters (P-B.4)
        • Choose the uppercase letter that matches: harder letters (P-B.5)
        • Find all the uppercase letters (P-B.6)

    • RF.2 With guidance and support, demonstrate understanding of spoken words, syllables, and sounds (phonemes).

      • RF.2.a With guidance and support, recognize and produce rhyming words (e.g., identify words that rhyme with /cat/ such as /bat/ and /sat/).

        • Choose the words that have a given ending (P-D.1)
        • Choose the picture that rhymes with the word (P-D.2)

      • RF.2.b With guidance and support, segment words in a simple sentence by clapping and naming the number of words in the sentence.

        • Choose the sentence that is spaced correctly (P-C.2)
        • Find a word in a sentence (P-C.3)

      • RF.2.c Identify the initial sound of a spoken word and, with guidance and support, generate several other words that have the same initial sound.

        • Identify the first sound in a word (P-E.2)
        • Which two words start with the same sound? (P-F.1)
        • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)

  • Phonics and Word Recognition

    • RF.3 Demonstrate beginning understanding of phonics and word analysis skills.

      • RF.3.a Link an initial sound to a picture of an object that begins with that sound and, with guidance and support, to the corresponding printed letter (e.g., link the initial sound /b/ to a picture of a ball and, with support, to a printed or written "B").

        • Identify the first sound in a word (P-E.2)
        • Which two words start with the same sound? (P-F.1)
        • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)

      • RF.3.c Recognize their own name and familiar common signs and labels (e.g., STOP).

        • Find the letter in the alphabet: lowercase (P-A.1)
        • Find the letter in the alphabet: uppercase (P-A.2)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: lowercase (P-A.3)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: uppercase (P-A.4)
        • Frequently confused letters: find the letter (P-A.5)
        • Frequently confused letters: find all the letters (P-A.6)
