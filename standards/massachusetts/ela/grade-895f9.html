W Writing

  • Text Types and Purposes

    • W.1 Write arguments to support claims with clear reasons and relevant evidence.

      • W.1.a Introduce claim(s), acknowledge and distinguish the claim(s) from alternate or opposing claims, and organize the reasons and evidence logically.

        • Identify author's purpose (8-C.1)
        • Organize information by main idea (8-J.2)
        • Distinguish facts from opinions (8-K.2)

      • W.1.b Support claim(s) with logical reasoning and relevant evidence, using accurate, credible sources and demonstrating an understanding of the topic or text.

        • Choose evidence to support a claim (8-K.3)
        • Identify supporting details in informational texts (8-K.4)
        • Identify supporting details in literary texts (8-K.5)
        • Classify logical fallacies (8-K.6)

      • W.1.c Use words, phrases, and clauses to create cohesion and clarify the relationships among claim(s), counterclaims, reasons, and evidence.

        • Transitions with conjunctive adverbs (8-J.3)
        • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (8-KK.1)

      • W.1.d Establish and maintain a formal style.

        • Which sentence is more formal? (8-C.2)

      • W.1.e Provide a concluding statement or section that follows from and supports the argument presented.

    • W.2 Write informative/explanatory texts to examine a topic and convey ideas, concepts, and information through the selection, organization, and analysis of relevant content.

      • W.2.a Introduce a topic clearly, previewing what is to follow; organize ideas, concepts, and information into broader categories; include formatting (e.g., headings), graphics (e.g., charts, tables), and multimedia when useful to aiding comprehension.

        • Compare and contrast (8-D.1)
        • Match causes and effects in informational texts (8-D.2)
        • Match problems with their solutions (8-D.3)
        • Identify text structures (8-D.4)
        • Order topics from broadest to narrowest (8-J.1)
        • Organize information by main idea (8-J.2)

      • W.2.b Develop the topic with relevant, well-chosen facts, definitions, concrete details, quotations, or other information and examples.

        • Distinguish facts from opinions (8-K.2)
        • Choose evidence to support a claim (8-K.3)
        • Identify supporting details in informational texts (8-K.4)
        • Identify supporting details in literary texts (8-K.5)
        • Classify logical fallacies (8-K.6)

      • W.2.c Use appropriate and varied transitions to create cohesion and clarify the relationships among ideas and concepts.

        • Transitions with conjunctive adverbs (8-J.3)
        • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (8-KK.1)

      • W.2.d Use precise language and domain-specific vocabulary to inform about or explain the topic.

        • Order topics from broadest to narrowest (8-J.1)
        • Describe the difference between related words (8-T.1)
        • Positive and negative connotation (8-T.2)
        • Determine the meaning of domain-specific words with pictures (8-X.1)

      • W.2.e Establish and maintain a formal style.

        • Which sentence is more formal? (8-C.2)

      • W.2.f Provide a concluding statement or section that follows from and supports the information or explanation presented.

    • W.3 Write narratives to develop real or imagined experiences or events using effective technique, relevant descriptive details, and well-structured event sequences.

      • W.3.a Engage and orient the reader by establishing a context and point of view and introducing a narrator and/or characters; organize an event sequence that unfolds naturally and logically.

        • Identify the narrative point of view (8-E.2)

      • W.3.b Use narrative techniques, such as dialogue, pacing, description, and reflection, to develop experiences, events, and/or characters.

        • Identify sensory details (8-E.1)

      • W.3.c Use a variety of transition words, phrases, and clauses to convey sequence, signal shifts from one time frame or setting to another, and show the relationships among experiences and events.

        • Transitions with conjunctive adverbs (8-J.3)

      • W.3.d Use precise words and phrases, relevant descriptive details, and sensory language to capture the action and convey experiences and events.

        • Compare passages for tone (8-C.3)
        • Identify sensory details (8-E.1)
        • Describe the difference between related words (8-T.1)
        • Positive and negative connotation (8-T.2)

      • W.3.e Provide a conclusion that follows from and reflects on the narrated experiences or events.

    • W.MA.3a Write short narratives, poems, scripts, or personal reflections that demonstrate understanding of the concepts of irony or parody.

  • Production and Distribution of Writing

    • W.4 Produce clear and coherent writing in which the development, organization, and style are appropriate to task, purpose, and audience.

      • Identify author's purpose (8-C.1)
      • Compare and contrast (8-D.1)
      • Match causes and effects in informational texts (8-D.2)
      • Match problems with their solutions (8-D.3)
      • Identify text structures (8-D.4)
      • Order topics from broadest to narrowest (8-J.1)
      • Organize information by main idea (8-J.2)

    • W.5 With some guidance and support from peers and adults, develop and strengthen writing as needed by planning, revising, editing, rewriting, or trying a new approach, focusing on how well purpose and audience have been addressed.

      • Organize information by main idea (8-J.2)
      • Use parallel structure (8-O.1)
      • Remove redundant words or phrases (8-O.2)
      • Use the correct frequently confused word (8-O.3)
      • Correct errors with frequently confused words (8-O.4)
      • Correct errors with signs (8-O.5)
      • Correct errors in everyday use (8-O.6)
      • Suggest appropriate revisions (8-O.7)
      • Identify plagiarism (8-P.4)
      • Is it a complete sentence or a fragment? (8-Z.5)
      • Is it a complete sentence or a run-on? (8-Z.6)
      • Is it a complete sentence, a fragment, or a run-on? (8-Z.7)
      • Combine sentences using relative clauses (8-AA.5)
      • Identify and correct errors with plural and possessive nouns (8-BB.5)
      • Identify and correct errors with compound and joint possession (8-BB.6)
      • Correct inappropriate shifts in pronoun number and person (8-CC.3)
      • Identify vague pronoun references (8-CC.4)
      • Correct errors with subject-verb agreement (8-FF.1)
      • Correct errors with indefinite pronoun-verb agreement (8-FF.2)
      • Identify and correct inappropriate shifts in verb tense (8-GG.3)
      • Commas with nonrestrictive elements (8-MM.2)
      • Commas: review (8-NN.5)
      • Use semicolons and commas to separate clauses (8-OO.1)
      • Decide whether ellipses are used appropriately (8-PP.3)
      • Correct capitalization errors (8-QQ.1)

    • W.6 Use technology, including the Internet, to produce and publish writing and present the relationships between information and ideas efficiently as well as to interact and collaborate with others.

  • Research to Build and Present Knowledge

    • W.7 Conduct short research projects to answer a question (including a self-generated question), drawing on several sources and generating additional related, focused questions that allow for multiple avenues of exploration.

    • W.8 Gather relevant information from multiple print and digital sources, using search terms effectively; assess the credibility and accuracy of each source; and quote or paraphrase the data and conclusions of others while avoiding plagiarism and following a standard format for citation.

      • Determine the main idea (8-A.1)
      • Choose evidence to support a claim (8-K.3)
      • Identify supporting details in informational texts (8-K.4)
      • Identify supporting details in literary texts (8-K.5)
      • Recognize the parts of a Works Cited entry (MLA 7th edition) (8-P.1)
      • Recognize the parts of a Works Cited entry (MLA 8th edition) (8-P.2)
      • Use in-text citations (MLA 7th–8th editions) (8-P.3)
      • Identify plagiarism (8-P.4)

    • W.9 Draw evidence from literary or informational texts to support analysis, reflection, and research.

      • W.9.a Apply grade 8 Reading standards to literature (e.g., "Analyze how a modern work of fiction draws on themes, patterns of events, or character types from myths, traditional stories, or religious works such as the Bible, including describing how the material is rendered new").

        • Determine the themes of short stories (8-B.2)
        • Compare passages for tone (8-C.3)
        • Analyze short stories (8-F.1)
        • Compare two texts with different genres (8-H.2)
        • Identify supporting details in literary texts (8-K.5)

      • W.9.b Apply grade 8 Reading standards to literary nonfiction (e.g., "Delineate and evaluate the argument and specific claims in a text, assessing whether the reasoning is sound and the evidence is relevant and sufficient; recognize when irrelevant evidence is introduced").

        • Compare and contrast (8-D.1)
        • Read and understand informational passages (8-G.1)
        • Trace an argument (8-G.3)
        • Compare information from two texts (8-H.1)
        • Identify supporting details in informational texts (8-K.4)

  • Range of Writing

    • W.10 Write routinely over extended time frames (time for research, reflection, and revision) and shorter time frames (a single sitting or a day or two) for a range of discipline-specific tasks, purposes, and audiences.
