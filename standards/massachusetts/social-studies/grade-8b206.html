History and Geography

  • 1 Apply the skills of prekindergarten through grade seven.

  • 2 Identify multiple ways to express time relationships and dates (for example, 1066 AD is the same as 1066 CE, and both refer to a date in the eleventh or 11th century, which is the same as the 1000s). Identify countries that use a different calendar from the one used in the U.S. and explain the basis for the difference.

  • 3 Interpret and construct timelines that show how events and eras in various parts of the world are related to one another.

    • Reading and creating timelines with BCE and CE (8-R.3)

  • 4 Interpret and construct charts and graphs that show quantitative information.

    • Identify shortage and surplus with graphs (8-G.5)
    • Identify shortage and surplus with data (8-G.6)

  • 5 Explain how a cause and effect relationship is different from a sequence or correlation of events.

  • 6 Distinguish between long-term and short-term cause and effect relationships.

Civics and Government

  • 12 Define and use correctly the following words and terms: Magna Carta, parliament, habeas corpus, monarchy, and absolutism.

    • Roman government: monarchy to republic (7-U.3)
    • The Middle Ages after 1100 (7-Y.3)

General Economic Skills

  • 13 Define and use correctly mercantilism, feudalism, economic growth, and entrepreneur.

    • Feudal Europe (7-Y.1)

  • 14 Explain how people or communities examine and weigh the benefits of each alternative when making a choice and that opportunity costs are those benefits that are given up once one alternative is chosen.

    • Costs and benefits (8-E.2)
    • Opportunity cost (8-E.3)

  • 15 Explain how financial markets, such as the stock market, channel funds from savers to investors.

  • 16 Define and use correctly gross domestic product, economic growth, recession, depression, unemployment, inflation, and deflation.

  • 17 Explain how opportunity costs and tradeoffs can be evaluated through an analysis of marginal costs and benefits.

  • 18 Explain how competition among sellers lowers costs and prices, and encourages producers to produce more.

    • What is a market? (8-E.5)

  • 19 Describe the role of buyers and sellers in determining the equilibrium price, and use supply and demand to explain and predict changes in quantity and price.

    • What is a market? (8-E.5)
    • Understand quantity supplied and quantity demanded (8-G.1)
    • Understand overall supply and demand (8-G.2)
    • Create and use supply and demand curves (8-G.3)
    • Identify shortage and surplus (8-G.4)
    • Identify shortage and surplus with graphs (8-G.5)
    • Identify shortage and surplus with data (8-G.6)

  • 20 Describe how the earnings of workers are affected by the market value of the product produced and worker skills.

  • 21 Identify the causes of inflation and explain who benefits from inflation and who suffers from inflation.

  • 22 Define and distinguish between absolute and comparative advantage, and explain how most trade occurs because of comparative advantage in the production of a particular good or service.

  • 23 Explain how changes in exchange rates affect balance of trade and the purchasing power of people in the United States and other countries.

  • 24 Differentiate between fiscal and monetary policy.

U.S. Economic Skills

  • 25 Explain the basic economic functions of the government in the economy of the United States.

  • 26 Examine the development of the banking system in the United States, and describe the organization and functions of the Federal Reserve System.

    • Banks (8-F.1)
    • Washington's presidency: part II (8-L.4)
    • Tariff, nullification, and bank war (8-M.2)

  • 27 Identify and describe laws and regulations adopted in the United States to promote economic competition.

  • 28 Analyze how federal tax and spending policies affect the national budget and the national debt.
