Showing alignments for:

  • District of Columbia Early Learning Standards: Pre-Kindergarten District of Columbia Early Learning Standards: Pre-Kindergarten

Actions

  • Print standards

3.0 Children communicate ideas and feelings, establish and maintain relationships with adults and other children through the literacy skills involved in I-Listening and Speaking, II -Reading, and III-Writing.

  • I Listening and Speaking

    • LL.3.I.1 Children comprehend oral directions and explanations.

      • LL.3.I.1.1 Follow directions of two or more steps.

      • LL.3.I.1.2 Demonstrate understanding of explanations.

    • LL.3.I.2 Children hear and discriminate the various sounds of language to develop auditory discrimination and phonemic awareness.

      • LL.3.I.2.1 Hear syllables in words.

        • How many syllables does the word have? (K-E.1)
        • Which word has more syllables? (K-E.2)
        • Sort by the number of syllables (K-E.3)

      • LL.3.I.2.2 Identify words that rhyme in songs, nursery rhymes, poems, and stories.

        • Choose the picture that rhymes with the word (P-D.2)

      • LL.3.I.2.3 Produce (make up) rhymes.

      • LL.3.I.2.4 Discriminate sounds as being the same or different.

        • Choose the words that have a given ending (P-D.1)
        • Which two words start with the same sound? (P-F.1)
        • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)
        • Which two words end with the same sound? (P-F.3)

    • LL.3.I.3 Children ask questions for a variety of purposes and answer questions of peers and adults.

      • LL.3.I.3.1 Ask questions to get information, ask for help, clarify something that is not understood.

      • LL.3.I.3.2 Answer questions with increasing detail.

    • LL.3.I.4 Children acquire and use increasingly rich vocabulary and language for a variety of purposes (receptive and expressive vocabulary).

      • LL.4.I.4.1 Use words to describe concrete objects, actions, and feelings.

        • Which feeling matches the picture? (P-K.1)
        • Choose the singular or plural noun that matches the picture (P-M.1)
        • Find the picture that matches the action verb (P-N.1)
        • Is the noun a person, animal, place, or thing? (K-EE.1)

      • LL.4.I.4.2 Integrate new vocabulary into conversations with peers and adults.

      • LL.4.I.4.3 Use complete and increasingly complex sentences.

      • LL.4.I.4.4 Describe concepts and past and future events.

      • LL.4.I.4.5 Add descriptive words to basic subject, verb, object sentences.

        • Use color words (P-L.1)
        • Use number words: one to five (P-L.2)
        • Use number words: six to ten (P-L.3)
        • Use number words: one to ten (P-L.4)
        • Compare pictures using adjectives (P-O.1)

      • LL.4.I.4.6 Ask Questions to acquire new vocabulary.

    • LL.3.I.5 Children participate in conversations.

      • LL.3.I.5.1 Engage in back-and-forth discussions about a topic with peers and adults.

      • LL.3.I.5.2 Initiate and/or extend conversations with peers and adults, using multiple exchanges.

  • II Reading

    • LL.3.II.1 Children understand and value books and other print materials.

      • LL.3.II.1.1 Listen to a wide variety of age appropriate literature read aloud.

      • LL.3.II.1.2 Initiate reading behaviors.

      • LL.3.II.1.3 Answer questions about stories and other print materials.

      • LL.3.II.1.4 Use books and other print materials to find information.

    • LL.3.II.2 Children demonstrate knowledge of and appreciation for books.

      • LL.3.II.2.1 Treat books with care.

      • LL.3.II.2.2 Hold books right side up and knows that books are read from front to back, top to bottom.

        • Identify book parts and features (P-I.1)

      • LL.3.II.2.3 Understand the concept of title, author, and illustrator.

    • LL.3.II.3 Children demonstrate understanding of print concepts.

      • LL.3.II.3.1 Know that spoken words can be written and read, and written words can be spoken aloud.

        • Find a word in a sentence (P-C.3)

      • LL.3.II.3.2 Know that print is read from left to right in English and many other languages.

        • Find a word in a sentence (P-C.3)
        • Identify book parts and features (P-I.1)

    • LL.3.II.4 Children develop familiarity with the forms of alphabet letters, awareness of print, and letter forms.

      • LL.3.II.4.1 Identify 10 or more letters.

        • Find the letter in the alphabet: lowercase (P-A.1)
        • Find the letter in the alphabet: uppercase (P-A.2)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: lowercase (P-A.3)
        • Choose the letter that you hear: uppercase (P-A.4)
        • Frequently confused letters: find the letter (P-A.5)
        • Frequently confused letters: find all the letters (P-A.6)

      • LL.3.II.4.2 Name letters in own name and in familiar words.

      • LL.3.II.4.3 Find specific letters in words in the environment.

    • LL.3.II.5 Children use emerging reading skills to make meaning from print.

      • LL.3.II.5.1 Use pictures as clues to the text.

        • Which feeling matches the picture? (P-K.1)

      • LL.3.II.5.2 Use different strategies (known words, knowledge of letters and sounds, patterns in text) to make meaning from print.

    • LL.3.II.6 Children comprehend stories and other texts.

      • LL.3.II.6.1 Engage actively in read-aloud activities by asking questions, offering ideas, predicting or retelling important parts of a story or informational book.

      • LL.3.II.6.2 Retell story events in sequence.

      • LL.3.II.6.3 Relate themes and information in books to personal experiences.

  • III Writing

    • LL.3.III.1 Children understand the purposes of writing.

      • LL.3.III.1.1 Dictate ideas and stories.

      • LL.3.III.1.2 Write to convey meaning.

    • LL.3.III.2 Children use emergent writing skills to make letters and words in many settings and for many purposes.

      • LL.3.III.2.1 Print own name.

      • LL.3.III.2.2 Make clear attempts to convey a message in writing.

      • LL.3.III.2.3 Begin to make letter-sound connections.

        • Put the sounds in order (P-E.3)
        • Choose the letter that matches the consonant sound (P-F.4)
        • Find the short a word (P-G.1)
        • Find the short e word (P-G.2)
        • Find the short i word (P-G.3)
        • Find the short o word (P-G.4)
        • Find the short u word (P-G.5)

      • LL.3.III.2.4 Use letter(s) to represent an entire work; use letter strings to represent phrases and sentences.
