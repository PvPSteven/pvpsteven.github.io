  • Vermont Early Learning Standards: Foundational Reading Skills Vermont Early Learning Standards: Foundational Reading Skills

Actions

  • Print standards

1 Foundational Reading Skills

  • 1.1 Children develop the foundational skills needed for engaging with print, reading and writing.

    • 1.1.A Print Concepts

      • 1.1.A.1 Indicate where to start reading on a page and how to move across and down a page.

        • Identify book parts and features (P-I.1)

      • 1.1.A.2 Demonstrate knowledge of the association between written words and spoken words

        • Choose the two words that are the same (P-C.1)
        • Find a word in a sentence (P-C.3)

      • 1.1.A.3 Display book handling skills

        • Identify book parts and features (P-I.1)

    • 1.1.B Phonological Awareness

      • 1.1.B.4 Segment syllables in spoken words with modeling and assistance

        • Identify the first sound in a word (P-E.2)
        • Put the sounds in order (P-E.3)

      • 1.1.B.5 Determine if two words rhyme

        • Choose the words that have a given ending (P-D.1)
        • Choose the picture that rhymes with the word (P-D.2)

      • 1.1.B.6 With modeling and assistance, segment onsets and rimes of single-syllable spoken words

        • Blend the sounds together to make a word (P-E.1)
        • Identify the first sound in a word (P-E.2)
        • Put the sounds in order (P-E.3)
        • Which two words start with the same sound? (P-F.1)
        • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)
        • Which two words end with the same sound? (P-F.3)

    • 1.1.C Phonics and Word Recognition

      • 1.1.C.7 Associate some letters of the alphabet with their specific sounds

        • Choose the letter that matches the consonant sound (P-F.4)

      • 1.1.C.8 Identify words that start with the same letter as their name

        • Which two words start with the same sound? (P-F.1)
        • Find all the words that start with a given sound (P-F.2)

    • 1.1.D Fluency

      • 1.1.D.9 Pretend to read a familiar book using language from the text and reading-like intonation
