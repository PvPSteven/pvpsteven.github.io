1 Congruence, Proof, and Constructions

  • Experiment with transformations in the plane.

    • 1.G.CO.1 Know precise definitions of angle, circle, perpendicular line, parallel line, and line segment, based on the undefined notions of point, line, distance along a line, and distance around a circular arc.

      • 1.G.CO.1.a Ability to fluently use mathematical vocabulary accurately

        • Angle vocabulary (G-C.1)

    • 1.G.CO.2 Represent transformations in the plane using, e.g., transparencies and geometry software; describe transformations as functions that take points in the plane as inputs and give other points as outputs. Compare transformations that preserve distance and angle to those that do not (e.g., translation versus horizontal stretch).

      • 1.G.CO.2.a Ability to make connections between function transformations (F.BF.3) and geometric transformations

        • Classify congruence transformations (G-L.1)
        • Translations: graph the image (G-L.2)
        • Translations: find the coordinates (G-L.3)
        • Translations: write the rule (G-L.4)
        • Reflections: graph the image (G-L.5)
        • Reflections: find the coordinates (G-L.6)
        • Rotate polygons about a point (G-L.7)
        • Rotations: graph the image (G-L.8)
        • Rotations: find the coordinates (G-L.9)
        • Compositions of congruence transformations: graph the image (G-L.10)
        • Congruence transformations: mixed review (G-L.12)
        • Dilations: graph the image (G-L.13)
        • Dilations: find the coordinates (G-L.14)
        • Dilations: scale factor and classification (G-L.15)
        • Identify transformation matrices (A2-G.15)
        • Transformation matrices: write the vertex matrix (A2-G.16)
        • Transformation matrices: graph the image (A2-G.17)

      • 1.G.CO.2.b Knowledge that rigid transformations preserve the shape of a figure

        • Translations: graph the image (G-L.2)
        • Translations: find the coordinates (G-L.3)
        • Translations: write the rule (G-L.4)
        • Reflections: graph the image (G-L.5)
        • Reflections: find the coordinates (G-L.6)
        • Rotate polygons about a point (G-L.7)
        • Rotations: graph the image (G-L.8)
        • Rotations: find the coordinates (G-L.9)
        • Compositions of congruence transformations: graph the image (G-L.10)
        • Congruence transformations: mixed review (G-L.12)

    • 1.G.CO.3 Given a rectangle, parallelogram, trapezoid, or regular polygon, describe the rotations and reflections that carry it onto itself.

      • 1.G.CO.3.a Ability to use appropriate vocabulary to describe rotations and reflections

        • Transformations that carry a polygon onto itself (G-L.11)

      • 1.G.CO.3.b Ability to use the characteristics of a figure to determine and then describe what happens to the figure as it is rotated (such as axis of symmetry, congruent angles or sides.)

    • 1.G.CO.4 Develop definitions of rotations, reflections, and translations in terms of angles, circles, perpendicular lines, parallel lines, and line segments.

      • 1.G.CO.4.a Ability to construct a definition for each term based upon a synthesis of experiences

        • Rotate polygons about a point (G-L.7)

    • 1.G.CO.5 Given a geometric figure and a rotation, reflection, or translation, draw the transformed figure using, e.g., graph paper, tracing paper, or geometry software. Specify a sequence of transformations that will carry a given figure onto another.

      • 1.G.CO.5.a Ability to interpret and perform a given sequence of transformations and draw the result

        • Translations: graph the image (G-L.2)
        • Reflections: graph the image (G-L.5)
        • Rotations: graph the image (G-L.8)
        • Compositions of congruence transformations: graph the image (G-L.10)

      • 1.G.CO.5.b Ability to accurately use geometric vocabulary to describe the sequence of transformations that will carry a given figure onto another

        • Classify congruence transformations (G-L.1)

  • Understand congruence in terms of rigid motions.

    • 1.G.CO.6 Use geometric descriptions of rigid motions to transform figures and to predict the effect of a given rigid motion on a given figure; given two figures, use the definition of congruence in terms of rigid motions to decide if they are congruent.

      • 1.G.CO.6.a Ability to recognize the effects of rigid motion on orientation and location of a figure

        • Translations: find the coordinates (G-L.3)
        • Translations: write the rule (G-L.4)
        • Reflections: find the coordinates (G-L.6)
        • Rotations: find the coordinates (G-L.9)

      • 1.G.CO.6.b Ability to use rigid motions to map one figure onto another

        • Transformations that carry a polygon onto itself (G-L.11)

      • 1.G.CO.6.c Ability to use the definition of congruence as a test to see if two figures are congruent

    • 1.G.CO.7 Use the definition of congruence in terms of rigid motions to show that two triangles are congruent if and only if corresponding pairs of sides and corresponding pairs of angles are congruent.

      • 1.G.CO.7.a Knowledge of vocabulary corresponding parts and the connection to the given triangles

      • 1.G.CO.7.b Ability to identify the corresponding parts of two triangles

    • 1.G.CO.8 Explain how the criteria for triangle congruence (ASA, SAS, and SSS) follow from the definition of congruence in terms of rigid motions.

      • 1.G.CO.8.a Ability to recognize why particular combinations of corresponding parts establish congruence and why others do not

  • Prove geometric theorems.

    • 1.G.CO.9 Prove theorems about lines and angles.

      • 1.G.CO.9.a Ability to construct a proof using one of a variety of methods

        • Proofs involving angles (G-C.8)
        • Proofs involving parallel lines I (G-D.6)
        • Proofs involving parallel lines II (G-D.7)

    • 1.G.CO.10 Prove theorems about triangles.

      • 1.G.CO.10.a Ability to construct a proof using one of a variety of methods

        • SSS Theorem in the coordinate plane (G-K.6)
        • Proofs involving isosceles triangles (G-K.10)
        • Proofs involving triangles I (G-M.8)
        • Proofs involving triangles II (G-M.9)

    • 1.G.CO.11 Prove theorems about parallelograms.

      • 1.G.CO.11.a Ability to construct a proof using one of a variety of methods

        • Proofs involving quadrilaterals I (G-N.11)
        • Proofs involving quadrilaterals II (G-N.12)

  • Make geometric constructions.

    • 1.G.CO.12 Make formal geometric constructions with a variety of tools and methods (compass and straightedge, string, reflective devices, paper folding, dynamic geometric software, etc.).

      • 1.G.CO.12.a Ability to use understanding of geometric concepts to establish a rationale for the steps/procedures used in completing a construction

        • Construct the midpoint or perpendicular bisector of a segment (G-B.10)
        • Construct an angle bisector (G-C.6)
        • Construct a congruent angle (G-C.7)
        • Construct a perpendicular line (G-D.2)
        • Construct parallel lines (G-D.5)
        • Construct an equilateral triangle or regular hexagon (G-G.5)
        • Construct a square (G-G.6)
        • Construct the circumcenter or incenter of a triangle (G-M.6)
        • Construct the centroid or orthocenter of a triangle (G-M.7)
        • Construct a tangent line to a circle (G-U.13)
        • Construct the inscribed or circumscribed circle of a triangle (G-U.17)

    • 1.G.CO.13 Construct an equilateral triangle, a square, and a regular hexagon inscribed in a circle.

      • 1.G.CO.13.a Ability to use understanding of geometric concepts to establish a rationale for the steps/procedures used in completing a construction

        • Construct an equilateral triangle inscribed in a circle (G-U.14)
        • Construct a square inscribed in a circle (G-U.15)
        • Construct a regular hexagon inscribed in a circle (G-U.16)

2 Similarity, Proof and Trigonometry

  • Understand similarity in terms of similarity transformations.

    • 2.G.SRT.1 Verify experimentally the properties of dilations given by a center and a scale factor:

      • 2.G.SRT.1.a A dilation takes a line not passing through the center of the dilation to a parallel line, and leaves a line passing through the center unchanged.

        • 2.G.SRT.1.a.1 Ability to connect experiences with dilations and orientation to experiences with lines

          • Dilations and parallel lines (G-L.16)

      • 2.G.SRT.1.b The dilation of a line segment is longer or shorter in the ratio given by the scale factor.

        • 2.G.SRT.1.b.1 Ability to develop a hypothesis based on observations

    • 2.G.SRT.2 Given two figures, use the definition of similarity in terms of similarity transformations to decide if they are similar; explain using similarity transformations the meaning of similarity for triangles as the equality of all corresponding pairs of angles and the proportionality of all corresponding pairs of sides.

      • 2.G.SRT.2.a Ability to make connections between the definition of similarity and the attributes of two given figures

        • Similar figures: side lengths and angle measures (A1-F.6)
        • Similarity statements (G-P.2)
        • Side lengths and angle measures in similar figures (G-P.4)
        • Similar triangles and similarity transformations (G-P.8)
        • Similarity of circles (G-P.9)

      • 2.G.SRT.2.b Ability to set up and use appropriate ratios and proportions

        • Similar figures: side lengths and angle measures (A1-F.6)
        • Similarity ratios (G-P.1)
        • Side lengths and angle measures in similar figures (G-P.4)

    • 2.G.SRT.3 Use the properties of similarity transformations to establish the AA criterion for two triangles to be similar.

      • 2.G.SRT.3.a Ability to recognize why particular combinations of corresponding parts establish similarity and why others do not

        • Similar triangles and indirect measurement (A1-F.7)

  • Prove theorems involving similarity.

    • 2.G.SRT.4 Prove theorems about triangles.

      • 2.G.SRT.4.a Ability to construct a proof using one of a variety of methods

        • Proofs involving triangles II (G-M.9)

        • Prove the Pythagorean theorem (G-P.15)

    • 2.G.SRT.5 Use congruence and similarity criteria for triangles to solve problems and to prove relationships in geometric figures.

      • 2.G.SRT.5.a Ability to use information given in verbal or pictorial form about geometric figures to set up a proportion that accurately models the situation

        • Congruency in isosceles and equilateral triangles (G-K.9)
        • Hypotenuse-Leg Theorem (G-K.11)
        • Similarity ratios (G-P.1)
        • Identify similar figures (G-P.3)
        • Side lengths and angle measures in similar figures (G-P.4)
        • Similar triangles and indirect measurement (G-P.5)
        • Perimeters of similar figures (G-P.6)
        • Triangle Proportionality Theorem (G-P.10)
        • Areas of similar figures (G-P.11)

        • Find trigonometric functions using a calculator (G-R.6)
