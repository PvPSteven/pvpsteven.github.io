LAFS.2.W Writing Standards

  • LAFS.2.W.1 Text Types and Purposes

    • LAFS.2.W.1.1 Write opinion pieces in which they introduce the topic or book they are writing about, state an opinion, supply reasons that support the opinion, use linking words (e.g., because, and, also) to connect opinion and reasons, and provide a concluding statement or section.

      • Use conjunctions (2-Z.1)
      • Use subordinating conjunctions (2-Z.2)
      • Use linking words to complete a passage (2-Z.5)
      • Complete the fact and opinion sentences (2-BB.1)
      • Complete the opinion passage with an example (2-BB.2)
      • Complete the opinion passage with a reason (2-BB.3)
      • Complete the opinion-reason-example table (2-BB.4)

    • LAFS.2.W.1.2 Write informative/explanatory texts in which they introduce a topic, use facts and definitions to develop points, and provide a concluding statement or section.

      • Select the detail that does not support the topic sentence (2-Y.1)
      • Choose topic sentences for expository paragraphs (2-Y.3)

    • LAFS.2.W.1.3 Write narratives in which they recount a well-elaborated event or short sequence of events, include details to describe actions, thoughts, and feelings, use temporal words to signal event order, and provide a sense of closure.

      • Use actions and dialogue to understand characters (2-Q.2)
      • Put the sentences in order (2-X.1)
      • Choose topic sentences for narrative paragraphs (2-Y.2)
      • Use time-order words (2-Z.4)
      • Choose the sensory details that match the picture (2-CC.1)
      • Add descriptive details to sentences (2-CC.2)
      • Revise the sentence using a stronger verb (2-CC.3)
      • Insert dialogue into a story (2-CC.4)
      • Use sense words (2-VV.1)

  • LAFS.2.W.2 Production and Distribution of Writing

    • LAFS.2.W.2.5 With guidance and support from adults and peers, focus on a topic and strengthen writing as needed by revising and editing.

      • Select the detail that does not support the topic sentence (2-Y.1)
      • Add descriptive details to sentences (2-CC.2)
      • Revise the sentence using a stronger verb (2-CC.3)
      • Combine sentences: subjects (2-DD.1)
      • Combine sentences: predicates (2-DD.2)
      • Combine sentences: subjects and predicates (2-DD.3)
      • Rewrite sentences using introductory elements (2-DD.4)
      • Is it a complete sentence, a fragment, or a run-on? (2-OO.6)
      • Identify and correct errors with plural and possessive nouns (2-PP.13)
      • Commas: review (2-YY.4)
      • Capitalization: review (2-ZZ.4)

    • LAFS.2.W.2.6 With guidance and support from adults, use a variety of digital tools to produce and publish writing, including in collaboration with peers.

  • LAFS.2.W.3 Research to Build and Present Knowledge

    • LAFS.2.W.3.7 Participate in shared research and writing projects (e.g., read a number of books on a single topic to produce a report; record science observations).

    • LAFS.2.W.3.8 Recall information from experiences or gather information from provided sources to answer a question.
