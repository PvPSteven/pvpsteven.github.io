Actions

  • Print standards

1 Communication: Learners communicate effectively in more than one language in order to function in a variety of situations and for multiple purposes.

  • 1.1 Interpersonal Communication: Learners interact and negotiate meaning in spoken, signed, or written conversations to share information, reactions, feelings, and opinions.

    • 1.1.1 communicate on very familiar topics using a variety of words and phrases that they have practiced and memorized.

      • 1.1.1.a greet and leave people in a polite way

      • 1.1.1.b introduce self and others

      • 1.1.1.c answer a variety of simple questions

        • Listen and choose the appropriate response to basic questions (S-1.Q)
        • Listen to questions about student life and choose the appropriate response (S-4.N)

      • 1.1.1.d make some simple statements in a conversation

      • 1.1.1.e ask some simple questions

      • 1.1.1.f communicate basic information about self and people they know

      • 1.1.1.g communicate some basic information about their everyday lives

        • Listen to questions about student life and choose the appropriate response (S-4.N)
        • Respond to plans and invitations by giving reasons and excuses (S-7.U)

  • 1.2 Interpretive Communication: Learners understand, interpret, and analyze what is heard, read, or viewed on a variety of topics.

    • 1.2.L Listening

      • 1.2.L.1 recognize some familiar words and phrases when they hear them spoken.

        • 1.2.L.1.a understand a few courtesy phrases.

        • 1.2.L.1.b recognize and sometimes understand basic information in words and phrases that they have memorized

        • 1.2.L.1.c recognize and sometimes understand words and phrases that they have learned for specific purposes

          • Listen to questions about student life and choose the appropriate response (S-4.N)
          • Listen to people talk about their school and everything in it (S-4.O)
          • Listen and match the statement to the image (S-5.R)
          • Listen to people talk about their families (S-5.S)

    • 1.2.R Reading

      • 1.2.R.1 recognize some letters or characters. They can understand some learned or memorized words and phrases when they read.

        • 1.2.R.1.a recognize words, phrases, and characters with the help of visuals

          • Weather and seasons (S-1.H)
          • Common adjectives (S-3.J)
          • Describe scenes from around school (S-4.Q)

        • 1.2.R.1.b recognize words, phrases, and characters when they associate them with things they already know

          • What is the conversation about? (S-1.R)
          • Cognates and parts of speech (S-5.J)

  • 1.3 Presentational Communication: Learners present information, concepts, and ideas to inform, explain, persuade, and narrate on a variety of topics using appropriate media and adapting to various audiences of listeners, readers, or viewers.

    • 1.3.S Speaking

      • 1.3.S.1 present information about themselves and some other very familiar topics using a variety of words, phrases, and memorized expressions.

        • 1.3.S.1.a present information about themselves and others using words and phrases

        • 1.3.S.1.b express their likes and dislikes using words, phrases, and memorized expressions

        • 1.3.S.1.c present information about familiar items in their immediate environment

        • 1.3.S.1.d talk about their daily activities using words, phrases, and memorized expressions

        • 1.3.S.1.e present simple information about something they learned using words, phrases, and memorized expressions

    • 1.3.W Writing

      • 1.3.W.1 write lists and memorized phrases on familiar topics.

        • 1.3.W.1.a fill out a simple form with some basic personal information

          • Listen and fill in a form with personal information: letters and numbers (S-1.P)

        • 1.3.W.1.b write about themselves using learned phrases and memorized expressions

        • 1.3.W.1.c list their daily activities and write lists that help them in their day-to-day life

        • 1.3.W.1.d write notes about something they have learned using lists, phrases, and memorized expressions
