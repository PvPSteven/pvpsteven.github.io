Actions

  • Print standards

1 Communication: Learners communicate effectively in more than one language in order to function in a variety of situations and for multiple purposes.

  • 1.1 Interpersonal Communication: Learners interact and negotiate meaning in spoken, signed, or written conversations to share information, reactions, feelings, and opinions.

    • 1.1.1 communicate and exchange information about familiar topics using phrases and simple sentences sometimes supported by memorized language. They can usually handle short social interactions in everyday situations by asking and answering simple questions.

      • 1.1.1.a exchange some personal information

      • 1.1.1.b exchange information using texts, graphs, or pictures

      • 1.1.1.c ask for and give simple directions

      • 1.1.1.d make plans with others

        • Ask questions related to plans and preferences (S-6.U)
        • Respond to plans and invitations by giving reasons and excuses (S-7.U)

      • 1.1.1.e interact with others in everyday situations

        • Question words: review (S-7.I)

  • 1.2 Interpretive Communication: Learners understand, interpret, and analyze what is heard, read, or viewed on a variety of topics.

    • 1.2.L Listening

      • 1.2.L.1 often understand words, phrases, and simple sentences related to everyday life. They can recognize pieces of information and sometimes understand the main topic of what is being said.

        • 1.2.L.1.a sometimes understand simple questions or statements on familiar topics.

          • Listen to statements about actions and habits related to food (S-6.R)
          • Listen to people talk about their favorite foods and drinks (S-6.S)
          • Listen to conversations about making plans (S-7.R)
          • Listen to people talk about sports, hobbies, and free time (S-7.S)

        • 1.2.L.1.b understand simple information when presented with pictures and graphs.

        • 1.2.L.1.c sometimes understand the main topic of conversations that they overhear

          • Listen to conversations about making plans (S-7.R)

    • 1.2.R Reading

      • 1.2.R.1 understand familiar words, phrases, and sentences within short and simple texts related to everyday life. They can sometimes understand the main idea of what they have read.

        • 1.2.R.1.a usually understand short simple messages on familiar topics

        • 1.2.R.1.b sometimes understand short, simple descriptions with the help of pictures or graphs

          • Read about famous people from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-2.Q)
          • Read materials related to school and student life (S-3.S)
          • Read about schools and education in the Spanish‑speaking world (S-4.P)
          • Read about families from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-5.T)
          • Time expressions to talk about frequency (S-6.M)
          • Read about typical meals and foods from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-6.T)
          • Time expressions to talk about the present and the future (S-7.K)
          • Read street interviews about plans, habits, and free time (S-7.T)

        • 1.2.R.1.c sometimes understand the main idea of published materials

          • Read about families from the Spanish‑speaking world (S-5.T)

        • 1.2.R.1.d understand simple everyday notices in public places on topics that are familiar to them

  • 1.3 Presentational Communication: Learners present information, concepts, and ideas to inform, explain, persuade, and narrate on a variety of topics using appropriate media and adapting to various audiences of listeners, readers, or viewers.

    • 1.3.S Speaking

      • 1.3.S.1 present basic information on familiar topics using language they have practiced using phrases and simple sentences.

        • 1.3.S.1.a present information about their life using phrases and simple sentences

        • 1.3.S.1.b tell about a familiar experience or event using phrases and simple sentences

        • 1.3.S.1.c present basic information about a familiar person, place, or thing using phrases and simple sentences

    • 1.3.W Writing

      • 1.3.W.1 write short messages and notes on familiar topics related to everyday life.

        • 1.3.W.1.a write information about their daily life in a letter, blog, discussion board, or email message

        • 1.3.W.1.b write short notes using phrases and simple sentences

          • Write sentences about yourself, your family, or people you know (S-5.V)
          • Write sentences that are true for you about sports, hobbies, and free time (S-7.V)

        • 1.3.W.1.c write about a familiar experience or event using practiced material

        • 1.3.W.1.d write basic information about things they have learned

        • 1.3.W.1.e ask for information in writing
