L.5 Language

  • Conventions of Standard English

    • Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage when writing or speaking.

      • L.5.1 Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English grammar and usage when writing or speaking.

        • L.5.1.a Explain the function of conjunctions, prepositions, and interjections in general and their function in particular sentences.

          • Use coordinating conjunctions (5-K.2)
          • Is the sentence simple or compound? (5-AA.8)
          • Is the sentence simple, compound, or complex? (5-AA.9)
          • Create compound sentences (5-AA.10)
          • Identify prepositions (5-HH.1)
          • Identify prepositions and their objects (5-HH.2)
          • Identify prepositional phrases (5-HH.3)
          • Identify coordinating conjunctions (5-II.1)
          • Identify subordinating conjunctions (5-II.2)
          • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (5-II.3)
          • Fill in the missing correlative conjunction (5-II.4)

        • L.5.1.b Form and use the perfect (e.g., I had walked; I have walked; I will have walked) verb tenses.

          • Choose between the past tense and past participle (5-FF.8)
          • Use the perfect verb tenses (5-FF.9)
          • Form the perfect verb tenses (5-FF.10)

        • L.5.1.c Use verb tense to convey various times, sequences, states, and conditions.

          • What does the modal verb show? (5-DD.2)
          • Use the correct modal verb (5-DD.3)
          • Is the sentence in the past, present, or future tense? (5-FF.1)
          • Form and use the regular past tense (5-FF.2)
          • Form and use the irregular past tense (5-FF.3)
          • Form and use the simple past, present, and future tense (5-FF.4)
          • Use the progressive verb tenses (5-FF.6)
          • Use the perfect verb tenses (5-FF.9)

        • L.5.1.d Recognize and correct inappropriate shifts in verb tense.

          • Correct inappropriate shifts in verb tense (5-FF.5)

        • L.5.1.e Use correlative conjunctions (e.g., either/or, neither/nor).

          • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (5-II.3)
          • Fill in the missing correlative conjunction (5-II.4)

    • Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English capitalization, punctuation, and spelling when writing.

      • L.5.2 Demonstrate command of the conventions of standard English capitalization, punctuation, and spelling when writing.

        • L.5.2.a Use punctuation to separate items in a series.

          • Commas with a series (5-KK.1)

        • L.5.2.b Use a comma to separate an introductory element from the rest of the sentence.

          • Commas with direct addresses (5-KK.3)

        • L.5.2.c Use a comma to set off the words yes and no (e.g., Yes, thank you), to set off a tag question from the rest of the sentence (e.g., It's true, isn't it?), and to indicate direct address (e.g., Is that you, Steve?).

          • Commas with direct addresses (5-KK.3)

        • L.5.2.d Use underlining, quotation marks, or italics to indicate titles of works.

          • Formatting titles (5-MM.1)
          • Formatting and capitalizing titles (5-MM.2)

        • L.5.2.e Spell grade-appropriate words correctly, consulting references as needed.

          • Use the correct frequently confused word (5-O.1)
          • Correct errors with frequently confused words (5-O.2)
          • Word pattern analogies (5-P.11)
          • Word pattern sentences (5-P.12)
          • Homophones with pictures (5-U.1)
          • Use the correct homophone (5-U.2)
          • Use guide words (5-Z.4)
          • Form plurals of nouns ending in f, fe, o, and y (5-BB.3)
          • Form and use plurals of nouns ending in f, fe, o, and y (5-BB.4)
          • Form plurals: review (5-BB.5)
          • Form and use plurals: review (5-BB.6)
          • Form the singular or plural possessive (5-BB.8)
          • Identify and correct errors with plural and possessive nouns (5-BB.9)
          • Form and use the regular past tense (5-FF.2)
          • Form and use the irregular past tense (5-FF.3)
          • Spell adjectives that compare (5-GG.8)
          • Pronoun-verb contractions (5-JJ.1)
          • Contractions with "not" (5-JJ.2)

  • Knowledge of Language

    • Apply knowledge of language to understand how language functions in different contexts, to make effective choices for meaning or style, and to comprehend more fully when reading or listening.

      • L.5.3 Use knowledge of language and its conventions when writing, speaking, reading, or listening.

        • L.5.3.a Expand, combine, and reduce sentences for meaning, reader/listener interest, and style.

          • Use coordinating conjunctions (5-K.2)
          • Choose the best transition (5-K.3)
          • Create varied sentences based on models (5-N.1)
          • Is the sentence simple or compound? (5-AA.8)
          • Is the sentence simple, compound, or complex? (5-AA.9)
          • Create compound sentences (5-AA.10)
          • Use the correct pair of correlative conjunctions (5-II.3)
          • Fill in the missing correlative conjunction (5-II.4)

        • L.5.3.b Compare and contrast the varieties of English (e.g., dialects, registers) used in stories, dramas, or poems.

          • Which sentence is more formal? (5-B.2)

  • Vocabulary Acquisition and Use

    • Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases by using context clues, analyzing meaningful word parts, and consulting general and specialized reference materials, as appropriate.

      • L.5.4 Determine or clarify the meaning of unknown and multiple-meaning words and phrases based on grade 5 reading and content, choosing flexibly from a range of strategies.

        • L.5.4.a Use context (e.g., cause/effect relationships and comparisons in text) as a clue to the meaning of a word or phrase.

          • Determine the meanings of similes and metaphors (5-E.4)
          • Interpret the meaning of an allusion from its source (5-E.5)
          • Vocabulary review: Identify story elements (5-G.3)
          • Vocabulary review: Analyze short stories (5-G.5)
          • Vocabulary review: Read and understand informational passages (5-H.2)
          • Find synonyms in context (5-S.2)
          • Find antonyms in context (5-S.4)
          • Which definition matches the sentence? (5-V.2)
          • Which sentence matches the definition? (5-V.3)
          • Determine the meaning of idioms from context: set 1 (5-W.1)
          • Determine the meaning of idioms from context: set 2 (5-W.3)
          • Find words using context (5-Y.1)
          • Determine the meaning of words using synonyms in context (5-Y.2)
          • Use context to identify the meaning of a word (5-Y.3)
          • Determine the meaning of domain-specific words with pictures (5-Y.4)

        • L.5.4.b Use common, grade-appropriate Greek and Latin affixes and roots as clues to the meaning of a word (e.g., photograph, photosynthesis).

          • Words with pre- (5-P.1)
          • Words with re- (5-P.2)
          • Words with sub- (5-P.3)
          • Words with mis- (5-P.4)
          • Words with un-, dis-, in-, im-, and non- (5-P.5)
          • Words with -ful (5-P.6)
          • Words with -less (5-P.7)
          • Words with -able and -ible (5-P.8)
          • Sort words with shared prefixes and suffixes by meaning (5-P.9)
          • Sort words by shared Greek or Latin roots (5-Q.1)
          • Use Greek and Latin roots as clues to the meanings of words (5-Q.2)
          • Use the meanings of words as clues to the meanings of Greek and Latin roots (5-Q.3)
          • Use words as clues to the meanings of Greek and Latin roots (5-Q.4)
          • Determine the meanings of Greek and Latin roots (5-Q.5)
          • Determine the meanings of words with Greek and Latin roots (5-Q.6)
          • Match words with Greek and Latin roots to their meanings (5-Q.7)

        • L.5.4.c Consult reference materials (e.g., dictionaries, glossaries, thesauruses), both print and digital, to find the pronunciation and determine or clarify the precise meaning of key words and phrases.

          • Use guide words (5-Z.4)
          • Use dictionary entries (5-Z.5)
          • Use dictionary definitions (5-Z.6)
          • Use thesaurus entries (5-Z.7)

    • Demonstrate understanding of figurative language, word relationships, and nuances in word meanings.

      • L.5.5 Demonstrate understanding of figurative language, word relationships, and nuances in word meanings.

        • L.5.5.a Interpret figurative language, including similes and metaphors, in context.

          • Similes and metaphors with pictures (5-E.3)

          • Interpret the meaning of an allusion from its source (5-E.5)

        • L.5.5.b Recognize and explain the meaning of common idioms, adages, and proverbs.

          • Determine the meaning of idioms from context: set 1 (5-W.1)
          • Identify the meaning of idioms and adages: set 1 (5-W.2)
          • Determine the meaning of idioms from context: set 2 (5-W.3)
          • Identify the meaning of idioms and adages: set 2 (5-W.4)

        • L.5.5.c Use the relationship between particular words (e.g., synonyms, antonyms, homographs) to better understand each of the words.

          • Homophones with pictures (5-U.1)
          • Use the correct homophone (5-U.2)
          • Multiple-meaning words with pictures (5-V.1)
          • Which definition matches the sentence? (5-V.2)
          • Which sentence matches the definition? (5-V.3)
          • Describe the difference between related words (5-X.1)
          • Positive and negative connotation (5-X.2)
          • Determine the meaning of words using synonyms in context (5-Y.2)
          • Use thesaurus entries (5-Z.7)
