RL.5 Literature

  • Key Ideas and Details

    • RL.5.1 Quote accurately from a text when explaining what the text says explicitly and when drawing inferences from the text.

      • Use actions and dialogue to understand characters (5-F.1)
      • Determine the themes of short stories (5-G.1)
      • Identify story elements (5-G.2)
      • Analyze short stories (5-G.4)
      • Identify supporting details in literary texts (5-L.4)

    • RL.5.2 Determine a theme of a story, drama, or poem from details in the text, including how characters in a story or drama respond to challenges or how the speaker in a poem reflects upon a topic; summarize the text.

      • Determine the themes of short stories (5-G.1)
      • Identify story elements (5-G.2)

    • RL.5.3 Compare and contrast two or more characters, settings, or events in a story or drama, drawing on specific details in the text (e.g., how characters interact).

      • Distinguish characters' points of view (4-F.3)
      • Compare and contrast characters (5-F.2)

  • Craft and Structure

    • RL.5.4 Determine the meaning of words and phrases as they are used in a text, including figurative language such as metaphors and similes.

      • Similes and metaphors with pictures (5-E.3)
      • Determine the meanings of similes and metaphors (5-E.4)
      • Interpret the meaning of an allusion from its source (5-E.5)
      • Vocabulary review: Identify story elements (5-G.3)
      • Vocabulary review: Analyze short stories (5-G.5)
      • Which definition matches the sentence? (5-V.2)
      • Which sentence matches the definition? (5-V.3)
      • Determine the meaning of idioms from context: set 1 (5-W.1)
      • Determine the meaning of idioms from context: set 2 (5-W.3)
      • Find words using context (5-Y.1)
      • Determine the meaning of words using synonyms in context (5-Y.2)
      • Use context to identify the meaning of a word (5-Y.3)

    • RL.5.5 Explain how a series of chapters, scenes, or stanzas fits together to provide the overall structure of a particular story, drama, or poem.

    • RL.5.6 Describe how a narrator's or speaker's point of view influences how events are described.

      • Identify the narrative point of view (5-E.1)

  • Integration of Knowledge and Ideas

    • RL.5.7 Analyze how visual and multimedia elements contribute to the meaning, tone, or beauty of a text (e.g., graphic novel, multimedia presentation of fiction, folktale, myth, poem).

      • Compare mythological illustrations (5-I.1)

    • RL.5.9 Compare and contrast stories in the same genre (e.g., mysteries and adventure stories) on their approaches to similar themes and topics.

      • Determine the themes of short stories (5-G.1)

  • Range of Reading and Level of Text Complexity

    • RL.5.10 By the end of the year, read and comprehend literature, including stories, dramas, and poetry, at the high end of the grades 4–5 text complexity band independently and proficiently.

      • Determine the themes of short stories (5-G.1)
      • Identify story elements (5-G.2)
      • Analyze short stories (5-G.4)
